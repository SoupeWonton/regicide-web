# Design assessments

Assessments are dated readings of a design snapshot. They are evidence and critique,
not canon; later decisions may invalidate their assumptions without erasing the record.

The rubric lives in [`../../canon/principles/golden-rules.md`](../../canon/principles/golden-rules.md),
with vocabulary defined by
[`../../canon/principles/depth-width-elegance.md`](../../canon/principles/depth-width-elegance.md).

- [`golden-rules-2026-06-18.md`](golden-rules-2026-06-18.md) — assessment of an early
  V3 draft, including a deliberately recorded relic-model contradiction.
- [`canonical-drift-blast-radius.md`](canonical-drift-blast-radius.md) — feature-by-feature
  impact map for changing any rule in the active canon manifest.

Scores are directional profiles, not targets to maximize. Tradeoffs between rules are
often healthier than a uniform score.
