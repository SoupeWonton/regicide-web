# Context Lore — Bones for Brainstorming

This is **bones, not a skeleton**: just the named
things and structural hooks the mechanics already imply, with **no lore written in**.
Everything here is a peg to hang a story on — invent freely. Nothing below is itself
lore; it's the list of slots that want lore.

> One-line premise the mechanics imply: it's a roguelite take on the card game
> **Regicide** — heroes fight up through a realm to **kill the crowned royals**
> (Jacks → Queens → Kings). "Regicide" = the killing of kings. That's the whole
> spine. Everything else is open.

## Named things that need a story (locked terms — keep the names)

- **Province** — one run / one region you push through. Numbered (Province 1, 2, 3…).
  Implied: a **Kingdom map** of regions you unlock and travel. *Why are there
  provinces? Who rules them? Why claim them in order?*
- **Act** — a leg of the journey inside a province. Three of them: **Jacks → Queens
  → Kings**. *What rises as you go deeper?*
- **The three climaxes** (these want the most flavor — they're the boss moments):
  - **Gate** (the Jacks fight)
  - **Courtyard** (the Queens fight)
  - **Throne** (the Kings fight — the final one)
- **Kingdom** — the meta layer / the world that persists between runs. The map, plus
  what you permanently unlock. *Whose kingdom? Are you taking it or saving it?*
- **Candle** — placeholder name for the currency you bank **when a run ends in death**,
  spent back in the Kingdom. **Name is up for grabs** — needs a lore-driven one.
  *What is a candle, in-world? What does spending one mean?*
- **Camp** — the rare rest stop on the road (resets your deck, lets you plan). The
  scarcest, most precious moment in a province. *What is a camp here? Who's there?*
- **The deck (52 cards)** — framed in design as your **life source / engine**, not a
  collection. *Is the deck the party's stamina? Fate? Something diegetic?*

## Tone pegs (only what the mechanics suggest — not prescriptive)

- Death is meant to feel like **"an opportunity, not a failure"** (you come back
  wider/stronger next run). A lore frame for *why* dying advances you would be gold.
- The fantasy is **personal engine-building** — a player should be able to say
  "I ran *this* hero + *this* build and it felt like mine." Lore that makes builds
  feel like identities, not stat-sheets, supports that.

That's the lot. Pick any peg and run.
