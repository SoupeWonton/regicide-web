## Decision 1 — Class = home-suit **path** + a separate selectable **Staff** *(Q31, Q5; amends 2026-06-25 C/D)*

The class model is clarified, **decoupling the path (ladder) from the Staff (passive):**

- **Each class has four suit paths** (♠/♥/♦/♣ ladders, per
  [`facet-and-linear-candidates`](../wiki/v3/classes/facet-and-linear-candidates.md)). A class
  **starts on its home-suit path**: **Sentinel ♠, Executioner ♣, Quartermaster ♦, Surgeon ♥.**
- **In V3.0 only the C2 rung of the starting path is live** (C3/C4/C5 rungs are not built). It is a
  **single ability — the ladder's first rung**: **entering Continent 2 grants it, revealed as an
  animation** *(Gab: how it shows)*, and it does **not** rung up within C2.
- **Completing C2 unlocks the other three paths** — they become **all open and selectable on
  entering Continent 2** on subsequent runs. *(This supersedes 2026-06-25 Decision D's "one
  player-chosen tree" — it is now all paths.)*
- **The Staff is a separate passive. Each class has four passive-signature Staffs (16 total); on the
  class-select screen at the start of C1 the player picks one of their class's four.** It is **not**
  the home-suit axis. *(This supersedes 2026-06-25 Decision C's "the Staff IS the home suit" framing
  and the "home suit is not a branch" claim — the home suit is the starting **path**; the Staff is
  orthogonal.)*
- **Staff swapping is the Fallen Heroes shrine** (Decision 2).

**The matrix is therefore 4 classes × 4 suit paths = 16 ladders**, with the **Staff as an orthogonal
selectable layer** — not a 4×4 with a Staff diagonal.

## Decision 2 — Fallen Heroes shrine: Staff swap, unlocked after C1 *(Q32)*

- **Verb:** switch your **Staff**.
- **Unlock:** completing **Continent 1** unlocks the Staff at the Fallen Heroes shrine. The shrine
  sits **mid-Continent-2** (per 2026-06-25 Decision E).
- **The swap offers one randomly-drawn Staff from each of the four classes** — one per faction, picked
  at random from that class's four signatures, **your own class included** (so a swap may also re-roll
  within your class). **It is just a swap — no cost, repeatable** (you can swap back on a later visit).
- *(2026-06-25 Decision E's "first visit = random faction Staffs" is the right shape; the refinement
  is that the randomness draws one Staff from each class's four-Staff pool.)*

## Decision 3 — Graft trigger and the royal value cap *(Q6, Q3, Q15)*

- A **graft triggers on an exact kill of an already-recruited card.** It **replaces either the
  suit OR the value (rank)** of one card in the player's hand — never both, never `+1`.
- **Replacement grafts are the only mutation vocabulary** for V3.0 (Q6 closed: the broader token
  catalog is retired until a playtest names a gap).
- **Royal grafts are value-capped at 10.** Exact-killing a Jack/Queen/King at a C2 gate **triggers a
  graft, but the grafted value is capped at 10** (a royal cannot push a card above value 10). This
  also answers the skipped-royal question: gate royal kills produce grafts regardless of the
  3/2/1 keep decision.

## Decision 4 — Forge and Sanctum split their verbs *(Q12, Q33; amends 2026-06-24)*

- **Forge = spells only.** The Forge's sole verb is now **upgrading spells by forging fragments**
  (Fragment → Half). It no longer rearranges grafts.
- **Fragments are pooled by encounter count** — the fragment supply available scales with the number
  of encounters played, not a per-drop coin flip. (Replaces the "~1 in 4 combats" placeholder.)
- **Sanctum = deck modification.** Sanctum takes over the **old Forge verb** — **rearranging grafts**.
  The budget is **two transfers per visit**, each moving a **suit or a value** between two owned cards
  (two suit transfers, two value transfers, or one of each); **no new power**, pure redistribution.
  This resolves Sanctum's dead "Attune" verb.
- **The road map needs retuning** to place the re-scoped Forge and Sanctum.

## Decision 5 — Spells: above immunity; held in the gauntlet *(Q36, Q29)*

- **Spells sit above matching suit immunity** — a ♦ spell is castable against a ♦-immune enemy.
  *(Gab: the visual treatment that signals this.)*
- The four-crystal holder is the **gauntlet** (name confirmed). *(Gab: the UI for the gauntlet,
  tiers, and "a Full is set aside.")* Whether holding all four pre-Full does anything stays open but
  is **not** a V3.0 blocker (Full is V3.5).

## Decision 6 — Equipment slots confirmed; mapping still to do *(Q30)*

- The **four relic slots are confirmed: Amulet · Ring · Cloak · Hat** (alongside the Staff).
- **Still to do:** map each relic candidate (Split Seal, Doorstop, Crown of First Claim, Black

## Decision 7 — Landmark roster ratified *(Q13)*

The roster proposed in [`proposals/systems/landmarks.md`](../proposals/systems/landmarks.md) is
**accepted**, with the Forge/Sanctum re-split of Decision 4 applied: **Forge** (spells), **Sanctum**

**Caravan** (pay-from-hand relic), **Camp** (fixed three-part rest — Decision 8), **Hunt** (C1 recruit
pursuit), **Shrine** (Consecrate), **Tower** removed, **Events** lore-only.

## Decision 8 — Forgiveness floor and Camp *(Q16)*

- **Opening guarantee:** the opening hand **always contains at least one Diamond** — no run can begin
  unable to draw.
- **Camp is a fixed three-part rest** (the recovery/rest landmark): reshuffle the discard into the
  Tavern, redraw a fresh hand to a **maximum hand size of 5**, and the **first attack deals double**.
  The whole bundle fires — not a pick-one-axis menu. (Magnitudes remain tuning.)

## Decision 9 — Product scope *(Q18, Q14, Q9, Q8, Q17, Q21)*

- **Solo only** for V3.0 (Q18).
- **Overdraw-and-select is kept as currently implemented** (Q14) — no change.
- **Tutorial is done** (Q9) — no further V3.0 tutorial design work.
- **UI/UX is Gab's call** (Q8); difficulty is **fine for now** (Q17). **Alpha contents to be

## Decision 10 — Continent content scope *(Q7)*

For V3.0 the **lore↔pressure mapping is 1:1** (Claim = C1, Shape = C2) and needs no further design.
The open part of Q7 — the **pressure packages, encounter rules, and loop scaling for C3–C5** — is
**deferred to V3.5** (with Q34 the God of Luck showdown).

## Decision 11 — Provinces are sub-continents *(Q37)*

A **province is a sub-continent.** Hierarchy: **Continent → Province (×3) → road → one boss.**

- **Each continent has three provinces; each province is one boss-tier** (one road ending in one boss).
- **C1 — Claim:** P1 = recruit **6s+7s** · P2 = **8s+9s** · P3 = **10s / Council of Tens**.
- **C2 — Shape:** P1 = **Jack** tier → Jack Gate (keep 3/4) · P2 = **Queen** tier → Queen Gate (keep
  2/4) · P3 = **King** tier → King Gate (crown, keep 1/4). The 3/2/1 pyramid is one tier per province.
- **Seams:** a **rest/reshuffle at every province boundary** (the forgiveness seam-reset applies at
  province seams, not only continent seams).
- **C2 path ability** reveals on **entering C2 = start of C2 Province 1**; **Fallen Heroes** sits at
  the **start of C2 Province 2**.
- **Save/resume: NONE in V3.0.** A run is **single-session**; only the **lineage meta** persists
  between runs. (This narrows the run-and-progression "multi-session" target to V3.5+.)
- **Code:** "province" replaces "chapter" as the sub-continent unit. Keep the province/gate machinery
  + `CONT1_CH*` maps; **closes the Q0 hold** — delete only the `provinceMode` *flag*, `CURATION_CUT`,
  and the non-ascending `else` branches.
