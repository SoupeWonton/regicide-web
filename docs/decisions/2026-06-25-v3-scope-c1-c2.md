# Design decision — V3.0 ships Continent 1 + 2; everything else is V3.5

> **Status: accepted (Landry direction; pending Gab ratification).** Cuts the shippable V3.0
> product down to the two acquisition continents and defers the back half of the run.
> **Amends** [`2026-06-24-crystals-continents-and-equipment.md`](2026-06-24-crystals-continents-and-equipment.md)
> (the five-continent run and forge-to-Full ending become V3.5) and
> [`2026-06-25-acquisition-cadence-and-royal-gates.md`](2026-06-25-acquisition-cadence-and-royal-gates.md)
> (in-run tree broadening becomes a between-run unlock). Source: 2026-06-25 Landry design session.

## Decision A — V3.0 = Continent 1 + Continent 2

The first shippable V3 build is the **two acquisition continents only**. Continents 3, 4, and
5 — and every question scoped to them — are **deferred to V3.5**.

**V3.0's victory condition is completing Continent 2** (defeating the first King at the King
Gate). The 2026-06-24 "forge all four crystals to Full → C5 God of Luck showdown" ending is a
**V3.5** concern; V3.0 does not need it.

**Consequence for crystals (resolved 2026-06-25):** the Full/win-token and the C5 showdown are
deferred to V3.5, but the **castable Fragment + Half spell layer ships in V3.0** — the two castable
rungs (Fragment = emergency, Half = strongest castable) plus the fragment-forge economy. Only the
non-castable **Full** rung and its endgame are deferred.

## Decision B — Death is no-comeback

Dying anywhere before the end of C2 **ends the run immediately** — full permadeath, no revival.
This coexists with front-loaded forgiveness: the guaranteed opening ♦/♥ engine floor and the
C1→C2 seam rest prevent *unfair* engine-stall deaths, but a genuine combat loss ends the
expedition. **The deck can lose a fight; it cannot soft-lock.**

## Decision C — Class = Staff (home suit) + a Tree of three suit-axis branches

Clarified/extended 2026-06-25. A class is a **Staff** plus a **Tree**:

- **Staff = the class passive, and it IS the class's home suit axis** (Sentinel ♠, Executioner ♣,
  Quartermaster ♦, Surgeon ♥). It maps to the live branch's existing class `abilityText`; reframing
  the passive as the Staff is the build path, not a rewrite.
- **Tree = the *other three* suit axes.** Each **branch converts the class's key moment into that
  suit's resource** (♠→shield, ♥→recovery, ♦→cards/draw, ♣→damage). The home suit is not a branch
  because it is the Staff. So Sentinel's tree = ♥/♦/♣; Executioner's = ♠/♥/♦; etc.
- **One branch unlocks per continent** — one at **C2**, one at **C3**, one at **C4**.
- **In V3.0 (C1–C2) only the C2 branch is live and playable.** The C3/C4 branches **and** every
  other class's tree are **visible but locked** (greyed teasers, not built).
- **The live "siege ultimate" (`siegeText`) is RETIRED entirely.** The C2 branch ability is newly
  designed; no siege reuse.

The regularization target is a 4×4 matrix (4 classes × 4 suit axes): the diagonal is each class's
Staff (home suit); the nine off-diagonal cells are the tree branches. Today only **Sentinel** is
clean (its three candidate ladders already are ♥/♦/♣); Executioner/Quartermaster/Surgeon each carry
a **redundant home-suit ladder** to replace (Warpath ♣ / Depot ♦ / Perpetuum ♥), and **no class yet
has a ♠ "→shield" branch** except Sentinel's home. See
[`../wiki/v3/classes/facet-and-linear-candidates.md`](../wiki/v3/classes/facet-and-linear-candidates.md).

**Open:** whether the C2 branch is **player-chosen** among the three suit axes (lean: yes — it is the
build choice) or fixed per class; and whether a branch is a single ability or itself rungs up.

## Decision D — Tree unlocks + Staff mix-and-match are the between-run meta reward

Completing C2 grants two meta unlocks (breadth, not stat power — consistent with the locked rule):

1. **One** other suit tree, **player-chosen**, startable on subsequent runs.
2. **Staff mix-and-match (post-C2 meta)** — pair one class's Staff with another class's tree ability
   (e.g. Sentinel Staff × Executioner's C2 ability). These combinatorial loadouts are the replay
   hook. **This is a post-C2 unlock, not the mid-C2 landmark.** The Fallen Heroes mid-C2 in-run swap
   is therefore *not* the source of mixing in V3.0 — mixing is earned by clearing C2 with a kept
   lineage (Fallen Heroes' V3.0 role, if any, is open).

Note: the **in-run** broadening described in the 2026-06-24 decision ("unlock the other suit
ladders over the run") was a C3+ specialization behavior and is **deferred to V3.5**. Within a
single V3.0 run a class uses its Staff + its one C2 branch.

## Decision F — Lineage is the meta vessel; wipe existing lineages

The existing **lineage** concept becomes the persistent meta-progression vessel. On the V3.0 cutover:

- **Clear all existing lineages.** They predate the new system and are invalidated.
- Players **create a new lineage**, shown a one-line explainer: *keeping your lineage unlocks
  additional content once you clear Continent 2* (the tree/Staff mix-and-match unlocks of Decision D
  attach to the surviving lineage).

This makes Decision D's meta unlocks concrete: completing C2 with a kept lineage is what banks the
player-chosen tree + Staff mix-and-match for future runs. (Implements the `current-state.md`
pre-alpha-saves lean: invalidate active runs, preserve only durable lineage identity going forward.)

## Decision E — Fallen Heroes appears mid-C2

The **Fallen Heroes** landmark (swap your Staff; the swapped-out Staff becomes available to other
classes) is placed **mid-Continent-2**. Swap cost, the legal cross-class staff↔ladder pairings,
and whether a swapped-out Staff is recoverable remain open (Q32). See Decision D's open item on how
this in-run swap relates to the post-C2 mix-and-match unlock.

## Deferred to V3.5 (do not scope for V3.0)
- C3 (Exploit), C4 (Adapt / the loop), C5 (Master / God of Luck showdown).
- The forge-to-Full win condition, the wager-flip animation, and self-weakening endgame.
- C4 re-crown; in-run tree broadening.
- Q7 (C3–C5 pressure packages), Q34 (C5 showdown), Q35 (C4 loop + opt-in ending).

## Explicitly deferred to a dedicated session (still V3.0-relevant)

  empty relic set; the full equipment brainstorm is its own session.
