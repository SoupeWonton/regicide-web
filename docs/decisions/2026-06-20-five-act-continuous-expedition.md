# Design decision — five-act continuous expedition

## Decision

The campaign is one continuous five-act run:

1. Claim through deliberate recruitment.
2. Shape a unique identity while completing acquisition.
3. Exploit that identity under focused pressure.
4. Adapt when the preferred line becomes unreliable.
5. Demonstrate mastery under telegraphed, sequenced pressure.

Death resets the expedition to Act 1. Acts can run independently only through internal
seeded fixtures for development and balance; players do not unlock later-act starts or
choose an arbitrary deck for Act 5.

The duration target is approximately one hour per act, four to five hours for a
successful multi-session expedition, 15–25 cumulative hours to first victory, and up to
roughly 200 hours for mastery across factions and meaningful permutations.

Death and milestone progression unlock breadth—classes/factions, relic possibilities,
routes, tradeoff-based starting alternatives, shortcuts, and permutations—not required
permanent statistical power. Exact unlock content remains a later decision.

## Rationale

The continuous deck is the history Act 5 tests. Direct act selection or free deck
construction would sever recruitment, graft, attrition, and adaptation from their
consequences. Session boundaries and save/resume address real-world time without
discarding that continuity.

The longer full-reset format requires strict fairness: later pressure is foreshadowed,
functionally lost runs resolve promptly, and replay longevity comes from divergent
possibilities rather than making one expedition longer.

## Consequences

- Five acts and their high-level purposes are no longer open design questions.
- Exact encounter packages, pressure mechanics, rest cadence, and numerical difficulty
  remain to design and validate.
- The current developed campaign shape requires migration.
- Act fixtures must represent plausible organic survivor states and remain unavailable
  as campaign progression.
- Class exploration must explain how each class expresses, exploits, adapts, and masters
  across the same five-act journey.
