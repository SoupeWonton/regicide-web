# Executioner

## Identity

Tier: 1 — Core Hero
Theme: Thresholds / Initiative
Suit: Clubs
Guiding question: When should the enemy die?

The Executioner is the threshold closer. This class turns almost-kills into actual kills and converts kill quality into campaign momentum.

## Expedition Stability Profile

| Pillar | Rating | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Shield | 0 | — |
| Access | 0 | — |
| Recovery | 0 | — |
| Initiative | 2 | reduces dangerous extra cycles and wrong-player exposure |
| Consistency | 1 | threshold precision lowers chaotic kill outcomes |

CT per chapter: ~0.5 to ~0.75
Specialization bonus: Hunter +0.5 / Duelist +0.25 (candidate for buff if prep economy dominates)
Primary failure mode addressed: wrong player owns initiative after a kill

Core role:
- secure kill turns without overcommitting
- convert precision into economy and planning advantage
- reduce dangerous enemy extra cycles

## Core Ability

Once per enemy, if damage leaves enemy at 1-2 HP, deal +2 finishing damage.

### What it actually does

- removes many awkward near-lethal outcomes
- saves cards that would otherwise be spent to finish
- lowers risk of extra counterattack turns

### Timing window

Best use cases:
- when enemy surviving one more turn is high risk
- when hand needs preservation for immediate next enemy
- in boss phases where cycle reduction is critical

Worst use cases:
- forcing suboptimal attacks just to trigger +2
- spending kill resources when overkill was already guaranteed

## Specialization Paths

## Hunter — Exact Kills

Exact kills grant one bonus preparation choice at next camp.

### Effect profile

- turns tactical precision into strategic depth
- rewards deliberate kill math and restraint
- strongest when camp options are high-impact

### Interactions

- Quartermaster Logistician: easier exact-kill setup via hand shaping.
- Commander: initiative control improves turn ownership for exact-kill timing.
- Boss preparations: more choices amplify this specialization significantly.

### Best play pattern

Road:
- prioritize exact kills on enemies where risk is manageable.

Camp:
- exploit extra prep choice to patch upcoming boss weakness.

Boss:
- spend prep advantage for phase control, not just emergency panic.

## Duelist — Initiative Control

Overkill on non-boss enemies grants +1 chapter currency.

### Effect profile

- rewards aggressive route tempo
- scales with road encounter count
- best when economy converts directly into preparation and support upgrades

### Interactions

- Warpath route commitment: high synergy due to elite density.
- Quartermaster Merchant: currency converts to better prep usage.
- Exile: thinning can increase high-damage consistency for overkill setups.

### Best play pattern

Road:
- overkill selectively on low-risk nodes to farm economy.

Camp:
- spend aggressively where boss pressure warrants it.

Boss:
- specialization provides little direct boss value; ensure road gains were banked effectively.

## Strategic Interactions

Suit interactions:
- Clubs: strongest direct synergy through threshold breakpoints.
- Spades: enables safer setup for precision kill turns.
- Diamonds: increases chance of finding exact threshold pieces.
- Hearts: offsets hand erosion caused by aggressive lines.

Campaign engine interactions:
- Death fork: faster kill closure lowers fork exposure.
- Camp planning: Hunter especially powerful due to prep economy.
- Difficulty arc: excellent for chapters where empowerment should feel earned.

## Best Team Context

Best alongside:
- Quartermaster for consistency
- Commander for sequencing
- Sentinel for safe setup windows

Risky alongside:
- no mitigation teams that overcommit damage
- campaigns with low camp leverage where Hunter cannot convert value

## Common Mistakes

- chasing exact kill at cost of survival margin
- wasting core +2 on trivial thresholds
- using Duelist lines when chapter economy already capped

## Practical Playbook

1. Map enemy thresholds before committing cards.
2. Use core +2 to save hand value, not showy damage.
3. Choose specialization based on chapter economy needs.
4. Convert road kill quality into camp leverage.
5. Enter boss with preserved hand depth from efficient closures.
