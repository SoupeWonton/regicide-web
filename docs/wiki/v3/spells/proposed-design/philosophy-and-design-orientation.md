# Spells — philosophy & design orientation

**Status:** Lead doc for V3 spells. Grounded in the
[[decisions/2026-06-24-crystals-continents-and-equipment|crystals, continents & equipment decision]]
(Decision A), which supersedes the earlier silver/gold/purple framing.

## What a spell is

**Four spell identities per expedition — one per suit** (♣ attack · ♦ draw · ♠ block · ♥ recover),
held in a **gauntlet** (working name) that carries exactly four — one per suit. A spell is a
**scarce one-use trump card** in a dedicated area: not shuffled into the Tavern, not counted against
the hand limit. Casting **consumes** it. At most one spell of each suit may be cast in a combat. You
can never hold two spells of a suit, and you cannot swap a suit's spell for something from a catalog.

## The crystal tiers

Each suit's spell is a **crystal** forged from suit-specific **fragments**:

1. **Fragment** — the floor; the suit's direct, castable emergency spell.
2. **Half** — forged from fragments; the **strongest castable** expression of the suit.
3. **Full** — forged further; **not castable.** It emits a strange light and is set aside,
   permanently unavailable for the rest of the run. The Full is the **endgame / win token**, not a
   power tier — forging all four is the opt-in path to the [[decisions/2026-06-24-crystals-continents-and-equipment|God of Luck showdown]].

**Only two rungs are ever cast** (Fragment, Half). Forging **consumes** its inputs (fragments → Half
→ Full); the spell transforms, it does not accumulate copies. Completing a crystal into a Full must
itself be **unlocked** (endgame-gated) — you cannot simply ask the Forge for one.

## The bar every spell must clear

- **One suit, one axis** — a spell restores the axis the encounter denied; it never becomes a
  generic effect or a second class loophole.
- **Spells sit above suit immunity** (current lean) — a ♦ spell is castable against a ♦-immune
  enemy, because its whole purpose is to give back a denied axis. The exception must be *visually
  explicit* (elevated frame, immunity-ignored preview).
- **No currency, no shop, no inventory construction** — four suit crystals with visible tiers,
  nothing shuffled into the deck.
- **The decision is "which axis to forge first, and which to deepen"** — plus the live tension of
  *cast now* (solve the emergency) vs *hold* (preserve fragments toward a Half, or toward the Full
  that retires the spell for the endgame).

## Orientation for this pass

For each suit spell, pressure-test:
1. **Identity** — is the Half unmistakably the *same* axis as the Fragment, just deeper?
2. **The moment** — does the Half create a new *trump moment*, or just a larger number (if the
   latter, the Fragment is enough)?
3. **Acquisition & forging** — fragments drop in combat (≈1 in 4); the **Forge** assembles them
   (fragments → Half → Full). Forge counts and drop rates are open.
