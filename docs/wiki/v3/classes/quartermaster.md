# Quartermaster

**Summary:** Quartermaster is the proposed combination and tempo class, using expanded hand and combination access to create decisive turns.

The **Quartermaster** is Kingfall's tempo and card-combination character. Its confirmed identity is to break or relax the normal **Combine** rules, allowing the player to hold, arrange, or play cards in ways other characters cannot. “Quartermaster gets extra draw” describes one current proposal and older implementation idea, but the exact draw bonus and full kit are not yet canon. (sources: [[canon/v3/classes/overview|Classes]], [[proposals/classes/four-core-classes|Four core classes]])

| Quick fact | Current answer |
|---|---|
| Character role | Tempo / large-turn setup |
| Owned loop station | Combine |
| Confirmed fantasy | Hold and combine cards beyond ordinary limits |
| Associated suit in proposals | Diamonds |
| Proposed starting hand | `3♦`, `4♦`, `5♦` with Provision |
| Proposed linear path | Requisition → Arsenal → Last Requisition |

## What the Quartermaster is

Kingfall uses a shared card economy: cards are attacks, combination pieces, payment against enemy counterattacks, and access to suit powers. **Combine** is the step where the player decides which legal cards form a single play. The Quartermaster is intended to manipulate that step—through hand capacity, combination limits, card reservation, or composition flexibility—so it can construct turns unavailable to other characters. (sources: [[canon/v3/core-loop|Core loop]], [[canon/v3/classes/overview|Classes]])

This is a tempo identity rather than simple card advantage. The Quartermaster wants to assemble the right cards and convert them into an unusually large or flexible turn before pressure becomes overwhelming. That distinguishes it from [[v3/classes/surgeon|Surgeon]], which keeps cards recurring over a long fight: Quartermaster values **quantity and arrangement now**, while Surgeon values **continued access over time**. (sources: [[proposals/classes/four-core-classes|Four core classes]], [[proposals/classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations|Facets and pressures]])

## Confirmed design

The Quartermaster is one of the four accepted V3 class identities. It owns the Combine station and represents tempo or big-turn play. Like every class, it will consist of a starting hand and one innate loophole that deepens during the expedition without a separate point currency or relic-slot tree. (source: [[canon/v3/classes/overview|Classes]])

The exact starting cards, extra draw amount, hand-cap increase, combination ceiling, and progression path are not confirmed. Diamonds are a strong proposal-level association because they draw cards, but “Quartermaster always draws +1” must not be presented as final canon. (sources: [[canon/v3/classes/overview|Classes]], [[proposals/classes/four-core-classes|Four core classes]])

## How Combine and extra draw work

In ordinary Regicide-style play, the player's current hand limits which combinations can be made and how much room remains for Diamond draw. Increasing maximum hand size is unusually powerful because one extra slot affects three budgets simultaneously: more possible offense, more cards available to pay a counterattack, and more empty capacity before a draw effect is wasted. This is why a Quartermaster hand-cap or extra-draw effect needs careful limits rather than being treated as a small convenience bonus. (source: [[research/strategy/additional-strategy|Additional strategy]])

Diamond effects draw toward the hand limit. A large draw at an already-full hand may produce little value, while drawing before payment or a large combination can reveal additional options. The proposed Quartermaster identity makes that timing more forgiving or more explosive, but the final Diamonds rule and exact class trigger are still unsettled. (sources: [[research/strategy/additional-strategy|Additional strategy]], [[proposals/classes/four-core-classes|Four core classes]])

Matched low cards are also relevant. Same-rank combinations can fire several suit powers together, so a group of low cards is not automatically weak. A Quartermaster that holds pairs, expands legal combinations, or changes Ace composition could turn seemingly minor cards into dense multi-suit plays. This is strategic evidence for the Combine identity, not accepted class text. (source: [[research/strategy/additional-strategy|Additional strategy]])

## Linear progression proposal

The fixed-path proposal gives Quartermaster a Diamond-oriented starting hand of `3♦`, `4♦`, and `5♦`, each associated with a proposed **Provision** effect. This starting package is intended to demonstrate card supply immediately, but neither the cards nor Provision are canon. (source: [[proposals/classes/four-core-classes|Four core classes]])

| Tier | Proposed effect | Intended experience |
|---|---|---|
| **Requisition** | Hand cap +2; first Diamond trigger each enemy draws extra | Hold more cards than normally legal and gain visible early fuel. |
| **Arsenal** | Stronger Diamond draw; combinations may total up to 12 instead of 10 | Assemble larger individual plays. |
| **Last Requisition** | Refill when the hand empties, potentially repeatedly | Dump the hand, reload, and create repeated large turns. |

These effects are an unaccepted proposal. Repeated refill and raised combination limits could dominate survival, draw, and damage simultaneously, so their frequency and numbers require playtesting against the [[v3/design/foundations/depth-width-and-elegance|width budget]] and persistent attrition. (sources: [[proposals/classes/four-core-classes|Four core classes]], [[research/strategy/additional-strategy|Additional strategy]], [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]])

## Facet proposal

The competing facet model keeps Combine as the permanent verb but allows one mutually exclusive expression during a run. (source: [[proposals/classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations|Facets and pressures]])

| Facet | Proposed identity | Example development |
|---|---|---|
| **Arsenal** | Construct exceptionally large present-turn plays | Raise combination ceiling, widen legal compositions, commit one decisive turn. |
| **Reserve** | Keep the correct answer for a future turn | Expand hand planning, reserve a card, recover it for a gate phase. |
| **Logistics** | Reconfigure how cards combine | Improve Ace flexibility, manipulate composition, revise a combination before committing. |

Arsenal emphasizes immediate output, Reserve emphasizes cross-turn planning, and Logistics changes card relationships. Only one facet would be active, preventing the Quartermaster from accumulating every form of hand control at once. (source: [[proposals/classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations|Facets and pressures]])

## Playing the Quartermaster through the five acts

During **Claim**, the player would learn the base Combine exception while recruiting low ranks. During **Shape**, the developing deck could indicate whether it needs larger plays, protected answers, or composition flexibility. **Exploit** would let that engine produce signature turns; **Adapt** would test whether it can function when ideal cards are unavailable; **Master** would require sequencing several large commitments without exhausting the expedition deck. This is the proposed facet/pressure interpretation, not finalized act content. (sources: [[proposals/classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations|Facets and pressures]], [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

Resource-compression pressure attacks the Quartermaster by hiding useful pieces deeper in the Tavern. Precision pressure asks it to combine toward an exact value rather than maximum output. Tempo rewards explosive turns but can punish waiting too long for a perfect hand. These interactions explain why “extra draw” is only one possible tool inside the broader Combine identity. (source: [[proposals/classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations|Facets and pressures]])

## Not yet decided

- Exact starting hand and whether those cards change permanent starting ownership.
- The base Combine loophole available in Act 1.
- Whether extra draw occurs once per enemy, once per round, or through another trigger.
- Hand-cap and combination-limit values.
- Linear progression versus facets.
- Whether refill can repeat and how it interacts with an empty Tavern.
- How Aces, same-rank combinations, replacement ranks, and effective suits are validated.
- How a Quartermaster is owned or shared in multiplayer. (sources: [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]], [[proposals/classes/README|Class workspace]])

## Implementation and evidence

The V3 Quartermaster kit is pending. Older builds and simulation work used a tier-one Diamond draw bonus, and older player feedback questioned what the Quartermaster actually did. Those facts identify a legibility and balance problem; they do not establish the final class. Historical simulation suggested Quartermaster was load-bearing in one three-player experiment, but the engine, class model, and campaign have changed enough that the result requires revalidation. (sources: [[delivery/current-state|Current state]], [[research/simulation/harness-notes|Harness notes]], [[research/playtests/raw/When I overdraw, I should be able t.txt|Raw feedback]])
