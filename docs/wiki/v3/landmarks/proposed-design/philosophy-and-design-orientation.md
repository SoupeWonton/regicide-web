# Landmarks — philosophy & design orientation

**Status:** Lead doc for the **proposed-design** pass — a fresh rethink of every landmark, to be
developed with Gab. The preserved *current* pages live under [[v3/landmarks/v2/index|landmarks/v2]];
these proposed-design pages start clean.

## Why landmarks exist

The expedition is **combat-heavy**: several fights build pressure, then a **short landmark stop
offers one sharp, telegraphed decision**, then combat resumes. A landmark is **one verb, one
decision** — never a generic shop or a reward buffet.

## The bar every landmark must clear

> **Why would *this* deck route here *now*, and what competing opportunity is surrendered?**

If a landmark can't answer that, it's a shop, not a landmark — cut it.

Constraints carried from canon:

- **No secondary wallet / no new subsystem** — landmarks spend resources already in the game
  (cards, the route choice itself, a fight), never mint a currency.
- **Conquest-first acquisition** — permanent cards come from the court or defeated enemies, not a
  generic draft or shop.
- **Acquisition stays capped** (~3 item-stops per act) so landmarks *punctuate* combat, not replace it.
- **Telegraphed** — the choice and its cost are visible *before* commitment.
- **Grounded in the real engine** — one royal at a time, card-economy attrition, the deck churns
  3–4× before a boss. A landmark's value must hold under those facts (e.g. foresight/scry effects
  are near-worthless, so no landmark should sell them).

## The orientation for this pass

Pressure-test each landmark on three questions:

1. **Necessity** — does it own a verb no other landmark, class, or relic already owns?
2. **Decision quality** — is the on-site choice one legible, meaningful fork (not a buffet)?
3. **Opportunity cost** — what does routing here actually cost (a fight skipped, cards spent, a
   different landmark missed)?
