# Five-act expedition

**Summary:** V3 is one continuous Claim–Shape–Exploit–Adapt–Master expedition whose deck history persists until victory or death.

## What it is

Claim teaches deliberate recruitment; Shape completes acquisition while revealing run identity; Exploit pressures the primary engine; Adapt tests secondary lines and fundamentals; Master sequences earlier pressures into a final foresight test. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

The first two acts are acquisition acts. Acts three through five pressure the conquered deck and class loophole without switching off the build or adding unrelated subsystems. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/structure|Campaign structure]], [[decisions/2026-06-20-five-act-continuous-expedition|Five-act decision]])

Players always begin an expedition at Act 1. Independent act entry exists only through seeded representative fixtures for development, balance, and UI testing. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

| Act | Dominant question | Relationship to the deck |
|---|---|---|
| Claim | Can the player recruit deliberately? | Establish low-rank ownership and precision literacy. |
| Shape | Can the run form an identity? | Complete acquisition and reveal class/graft direction. |
| Exploit | Can the engine express its strength? | Push the primary line under focused pressure. |
| Adapt | Can fundamentals support a secondary line? | Disrupt preference without disabling the build. |
| Master | Can the player handle sequenced pressure? | Synthesize the expedition's learned tests. |

The same hand, Tavern, discard, conquered cards, grafts, class identity, and consequences cross these acts. This makes later content an examination of earlier judgment and gives [[v3/stages/death-and-meta-progression|full-reset death]] meaning. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]], [[decisions/2026-06-20-five-act-continuous-expedition|Five-act decision]])

The frame is canon; encounter counts, roads, pressure packages, gates, and rests are not. Internal fixtures should represent plausible weak, median, strong, narrow, and attrition-damaged survivor states without becoming player starts. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/structure|Campaign structure]], [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]])

## Confirmed design

The five act names, high-level purposes, continuous state, full-reset death, Act 1 player start, save/resume support, and internal-only act fixtures are canon. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]], [[decisions/2026-06-20-five-act-continuous-expedition|Five-act decision]])

## Not yet decided

Exact maps, encounter counts, pressure packages, gates, rests, class deepening moments, and numerical difficulty remain open. (source: [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]])
