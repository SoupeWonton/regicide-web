# Five-beat expedition (five continents)

**Summary:** V3 is one continuous Claim–Shape–Exploit–Adapt–Master expedition, themed as **five continents** with a God of Luck lore layer and an **opt-in ending**; the deck history persists until victory or death.

## What it is

Claim teaches deliberate recruitment; Shape completes acquisition while revealing run identity; Exploit pressures the primary engine; Adapt tests secondary lines and fundamentals; Master sequences earlier pressures into a final foresight test. The five beats map **1:1 to Continents 1–5**. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

The first two continents are acquisition. Continents three through five pressure the conquered deck and class loophole without switching off the build. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/structure|Campaign structure]], [[decisions/2026-06-24-crystals-continents-and-equipment|Crystals decision]])

Players always begin an expedition at Continent 1. Independent beat entry exists only through seeded representative fixtures for development, balance, and UI testing. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

**Lore & ending.** Recruited by the God of Luck, the player defeats successive kings (C1–C3); **Continent 4 loops** indefinitely (you always lose the wager, having become the king). The run is finished **only by choice** — forging all four [spell crystals](../mechanics/items-and-power-vehicles.md) to **Full** strips the player's spells and opens the **Continent 5** God of Luck showdown. See [[decisions/2026-06-24-crystals-continents-and-equipment|the decision]] for the full overlay.

| Beat / Continent | Dominant question                          | Relationship to the deck                               |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Claim · C1       | Can the player recruit deliberately?       | Establish low-rank ownership and precision literacy.   |
| Shape · C2       | Can the run form an identity?              | Complete acquisition and reveal class/graft direction. |
| Exploit · C3     | Can the engine express its strength?       | Push the primary line under focused pressure.          |
| Adapt · C4       | Can fundamentals support a secondary line? | Disrupt preference without disabling the build; the loop. |
| Master · C5      | Can the player handle sequenced pressure?  | The opt-in God of Luck showdown; synthesize the learned tests. |

The same hand, Tavern, discard, conquered cards, grafts, class identity, and consequences cross these continents. This makes later content an examination of earlier judgment and gives [[v3/stages/death-and-meta-progression|full-reset death]] meaning. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]], [[decisions/2026-06-24-crystals-continents-and-equipment|Crystals decision]])

The frame is canon; encounter counts, roads, pressure packages, gates, and rests are not. Internal fixtures should represent plausible weak, median, strong, narrow, and attrition-damaged survivor states without becoming player starts. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/structure|Campaign structure]], [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]])

## Confirmed design

The five beat names, high-level purposes, continuous state, full-reset death, Continent 1 player start, save/resume support, the continent + God of Luck overlay, the opt-in (forge-to-Full) ending, and internal-only fixtures are canon. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]], [[decisions/2026-06-24-crystals-continents-and-equipment|Crystals decision]])

## Not yet decided

Exact maps, encounter counts, pressure packages, gates, rests, class deepening moments, and numerical difficulty remain open. (source: [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]])
