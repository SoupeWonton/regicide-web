# Act pressure model

**Summary:** Act purposes are accepted, but the pressure packages, ordering, gates, rests, and encounter rules that realize them remain under design.

## What it is

The accepted act frame progresses from acquisition through expression, disruption, and synthesis. Later pressure must be telegraphed, sequenced across time, and capable of testing every class without invalidating one by fiat. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]], [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]])

Exploratory pressure packages include attrition, tempo, adaptation, precision, and resource compression. Proposed permutations such as The Long War or The Shifting Crown vary Acts 3–5 while preserving Claim and Shape. These are examples, not accepted campaign content. (source: [[proposals/classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations|Facets and pressures]])

Royal sequences, immunity, gate rewards, rest cadence, difficulty curves, and representative act-entry fixtures remain open. (source: [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]])

The exploratory packages ask distinct questions through existing card state: **attrition** restricts recovery; **tempo** escalates visible danger; **adaptation** makes a preferred pattern unfavorable; **precision** conflicts survival with exact-kill setup; and **resource compression** limits immediate access to useful cards. They become content only when encounters express the pressure without adding an economy or invalidating a class. (sources: [[proposals/classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations|Facets and pressures]], [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]])

The Long War emphasizes attrition and compression; The Rising Court emphasizes tempo and precision; The Shifting Crown emphasizes rotating suit pressure; The Broken Road reduces acquisition and tests recovery. These permutations demonstrate possible breadth, not canonical named campaigns. (source: [[proposals/classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations|Facets and pressures]])

Pressure and [[v3/classes/class-progression-model|class expression]] must be evaluated together. Every class needs moments to exploit its engine and later moments requiring adaptation; the Master beat (Continent 5, the opt-in God of Luck showdown) should sequence tests across time so mastery remains foresight rather than simultaneous bookkeeping. (sources: [[proposals/classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations|Facets and pressures]], [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]])

## Confirmed design

Claim, Shape, Exploit, Adapt, and Master are accepted purposes. Pressure must be visible, sequenced, and avoid invalidating a class or adding a parallel subsystem. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]], [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]])

## Current design work

Attrition, tempo, adaptation, precision, and resource compression are exploratory packages. The Long War, Rising Court, Shifting Crown, and Broken Road are example permutations, not canon. (source: [[proposals/classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations|Facets and pressures]])

## Not yet decided

Package ownership by act, encounter rules, royal sequences, rest cadence, difficulty curve, fixtures, and final permutation structure remain unresolved. (source: [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]])
