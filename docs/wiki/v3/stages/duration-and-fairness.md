# Duration and fairness

**Summary:** The long expedition is viable only if act boundaries provide closure, losses remain attributable, and mastery accelerates decisions without skipping conquest.

## What it is

The target is about one hour per act for deliberate early play, compressing toward 35–45 minutes with mastery and riskier routes. A winning expedition targets four to five hours across sessions, with first victory around 15–25 cumulative hours and long-term breadth potentially supporting about 200 hours. These are playtest targets, not content quotas. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

Act boundaries need a recap and safe resume point. Late pressure must be foreshadowed, and a functionally lost run should resolve promptly or offer a genuine recovery plan rather than consuming another hour before admitting failure. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

Mastery may shorten familiar decisions and unlock risky routes, but it never grants direct later-act entry. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

These targets impose a fairness contract. After several hours, failure must trace to visible decisions and introduced pressure; Act 5 cannot reveal a surprise rule that invalidates the journey. Protecting time does not guarantee victory—it requires communicated danger and meaningful response. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

One-hour acts need emotional closure as well as saving. A recap should show how the deck changed, which pressures were survived, and what state carries forward, linking [[v3/design/product/ui-ux-contract|UI trust]] to [[v3/mechanics/persistent-expedition-deck|continuity]]. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

The 200-hour aspiration must come from classes, facets, routes, relic possibilities, and permutations rather than padding a four-to-five-hour expedition. Mastery should speed familiar reasoning while creating riskier choices. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

## Confirmed design

Targets are roughly one hour per deliberate act, 35–45 minutes for mastered acts, four to five hours for a winning expedition, 15–25 cumulative hours to first victory, and up to roughly 200 hours of breadth-based mastery. Act boundaries provide recap and safe resume. (source: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

## Not yet decided

Encounter counts, conversion rates, exact late-run death targets, collapse detection, faster routes, and the content breadth required to sustain mastery remain open to validation. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]], [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]])
