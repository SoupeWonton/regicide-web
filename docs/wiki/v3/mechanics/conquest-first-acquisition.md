# Conquest-first acquisition

**Summary:** Permanent cards enter through the starting court or defeated enemies rather than generic drafts, purchases, or automatic rewards.

## What it is

Conquest is the defining acquisition rule: the player should be able to connect an acquired card to an enemy they fought. Route choices may influence available enemies, but menus must not replace combat and precision as the source. (sources: [[canon/v3/vision|V3 vision]], [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

Number enemies are ordinary growth targets. Royals currently serve as gate bosses rather than ordinary recruits, while the Ace begins as a low-rank companion. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/structure|Campaign structure]], [[canon/v3/core-loop|Core loop]])

Recurrence and recovery remain open. Natural recurrence, earned Hunts, gate challenges, automatic backfill, and no safety net represent different degrees of agency and consequence; none is fully accepted. (source: [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

Acquisition matters because it makes enemy selection, routing, and exact-value play part of deckbuilding. A generic offer asks which card is strongest; conquest asks whether the player can create and finish the fight that earns it, tying [[v3/stages/act-pressure-model|encounter content]] directly to progression. (sources: [[canon/v3/vision|V3 vision]], [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

The recovery spectrum changes the meaning of failure. A single chance maximizes consequence but risks a death spiral; natural recurrence offers uncertain second chances; a Hunt sacrifices another route reward for pursuit; gate recovery stabilizes the floor; automatic backfill is reliable but least owned. Any chosen model must preserve combat and precision as the source. (source: [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

Royals require separate design because current V3 makes them gate bosses rather than ordinary recruits. Old tutorial and simulation conclusions about royal bomb-cards cannot silently define their new payoff. (sources: [[canon/v3/campaign/structure|Campaign structure]], [[proposals/player-experience/tutorial|Tutorial proposal]], [[research/simulation/harness-notes|Harness notes]])

## Confirmed design

Permanent cards come from the starting court or defeated enemies. Number enemies are ordinary acquisition targets, an exact kill is required for recruitment, and no generic shop or draft replaces conquest. Royals are currently gate bosses rather than ordinary recruits. (sources: [[canon/v3/vision|V3 vision]], [[canon/v3/campaign/structure|Campaign structure]])

## Current design work

Natural recurrence, directed Hunts, gate recovery, and backfill are competing ways to prevent missed recruits from producing a doomed run. None is accepted. (source: [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

## Not yet decided

Starting ownership, recurrence frequency, recovery cost, royal payoff, and the final relationship between acquisition and permanent curation remain unresolved. (source: [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]])
