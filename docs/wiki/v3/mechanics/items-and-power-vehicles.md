# Items and power vehicles

**Summary:** V3 canon currently permits rare relics and removes standalone spells, while an active proposal explores exactly four consumable golden spell cards and a focused relic test pool.

Kingfall's accepted V3 design concentrates lasting power in the conquered deck and very rare relics. An active proposal now explores four suit-aligned, one-use golden spell cards as emergency trump cards; that proposal reopens, but has not yet replaced, the canonical removal of standalone spells. (sources: [[canon/v3/systems/items|Items]], [[proposals/systems/relics-and-spell-cards|Relics and spell cards proposal]])

| Quick fact | Current answer |
|---|---|
| Confirmed lasting vehicles | Conquered cards, replacement grafts, and very rare relics |
| Relic model | Slotless, sparse, run-defining exceptions |
| Canonical spell status | Standalone inventory removed |
| Active spell proposal | Exactly four one-use golden cards, one per suit |
| Proposed spell progression | Standard → rare → possible mythic rare |
| Spell restoration | None |

## Confirmed design

Relics are rare, slotless exceptions rather than class progression or a managed equipment grid. Most runs should not accumulate a routine inventory of them, and any relic must change an attributable decision rather than provide invisible decoration. (sources: [[canon/v3/systems/items|Items]], [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]], [[decisions/2026-06-18-v3-foundation|V3 foundation]])

Canon currently removes standalone spell inventory and spell currency, directing burst effects toward cards or existing combat actions. The golden-card model below is therefore a proposal requiring an explicit decision before it becomes V3 canon. (sources: [[canon/v3/systems/items|Items]], [[proposals/systems/relics-and-spell-cards|Relics and spell cards proposal]])

## Current spell-card proposal

The proposed system contains exactly four spell identities: [[v3/spells/clubs-spell|Clubs]], [[v3/spells/diamonds-spell|Diamonds]], [[v3/spells/spades-spell|Spades]], and [[v3/spells/hearts-spell|Hearts]]. A player may hold at most one of each. Acquiring an empty suit gives its standard card; acquiring that suit again while holding it upgrades the same card to rare and potentially mythic rare. Casting consumes the card completely—there is no recharge, restoration, or exhaustion state. (source: [[proposals/systems/relics-and-spell-cards|Relics and spell cards proposal]])

Their golden presentation distinguishes them from the conquered court. Suit communicates the emergency axis—attack, draw, block, or recovery—while the current design lean lets spell text operate through matching enemy immunity. Otherwise, the emergency Diamond card would fail against the enemy producing the draw crisis it exists to answer. This immunity exception remains proposed, not confirmed. (source: [[proposals/systems/relics-and-spell-cards|Relics and spell cards proposal]])

Holding a spell is also an upgrade decision. Spending a standard card solves the present fight but forfeits the opportunity to carry that instance upward to rare or mythic. This makes the spell both a get-out-of-jail card and a visible investment the player may sacrifice under pressure. (source: [[proposals/systems/relics-and-spell-cards|Relics and spell cards proposal]])

## Current relic proposal

The clearest new candidates are [[v3/relics/split-seal|Split Seal]], [[v3/relics/crown-of-first-claim|Crown of First Claim]], [[v3/relics/doorstop|Doorstop]], and [[v3/relics/black-standard|Black Standard]]. Four effective V2 relics—[[v3/relics/sainted-scalpel|Sainted Scalpel]], [[v3/relics/combat-cache|Combat Cache]], [[v3/relics/warhorn|Warhorn]], and [[v3/relics/hoard|Hoard]]—remain comparison candidates rather than accepted carryovers. (source: [[proposals/systems/relics-and-spell-cards|Relics and spell cards proposal]])

The emerging test is practical: a relic must create usable cards, enable otherwise impossible exact kills or blocks, or deliberately shape deck identity. Effects based only on Tavern order failed this test because high card churn and immediate survival make access more valuable than foreknowledge. (source: [[proposals/systems/relics-and-spell-cards|Relics and spell cards proposal]])

## Acquisition under discussion

The [[v3/landmarks/market|Market or Caravan]] may charge current hand value instead of gold: discard cards meeting a visible total to acquire or upgrade a spell, with values such as 5 for an early spell and 10 for a later rare under consideration. Costly relics may also appear there without requiring relic-for-relic exchange. [[v3/landmarks/lair|Lairs]] remain the strongest proposed source for relics earned through dangerous combat. (source: [[proposals/systems/relics-and-spell-cards|Relics and spell cards proposal]])

## Not yet decided

- Exact standard, rare, and mythic spell text.
- Whether spells formally override matching immunity.
- Whether reacquiring a consumed upgraded suit starts again at standard.
- Whether Abbey/Sanctum or Market/Caravan is the primary spell source.
- Exact Market discard totals and relic prices.
- Final relic catalog, opportunity count, and power baseline.
- Which parts of this proposal supersede the current spell-removal canon. (source: [[proposals/systems/relics-and-spell-cards|Relics and spell cards proposal]])
