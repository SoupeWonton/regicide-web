# Deck lifecycle

**Summary:** The lifecycle asks how a conquered card enters, changes, recurs, leaves, and gains meaning during one persistent expedition.

## What it is

Accepted foundations include conquest-first acquisition, exact-kill recruitment or replacement, number enemies as ordinary targets, deck persistence, one primary card engine, recognizable rank/suit vocabulary, and no fragment wallet. Permanent removal is open. (sources: [[canon/v3/constraints|V3 constraints]], [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

Four axes remain unresolved: recruitment opportunity, starting ownership, missed-acquisition recovery, and curation/removal. Detailed replacement questions form a fifth dependency even though replacement semantics themselves are accepted. (source: [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

Three coherent models are being compared: irreversible conquest, earned curation, and transformative resilience. Earned curation is the suggested lead for exploration, with irreversible conquest as a control; this recommendation is not canon. (source: [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

Five proposed pillars test every model: the battlefield remains the source; exact kills write visible history; the deck carries consequences; consistency is player-authored through a tradeoff; and lifecycle tools remain inside one legible card engine. A recovery feature can preserve viability while still failing if it silently grants the missing card. (source: [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

| Axis | Alternatives | Central tension |
|---|---|---|
| Recruitment | Single chance, natural recurrence, pursued recurrence, gate recovery | Consequence versus variance |
| Starting ownership | Shared court, shared plus class cards, distinct decks | Comparable curve versus immediate identity |
| Recovery | None, Hunt, gate challenge, backfill, adaptive pressure | Owned comeback versus normalization |
| Curation | None, suppression, retirement, transformation, broad removal | Conquest history versus consistency |

Irreversible conquest is the clean control but risks dilution and doomed runs. Earned curation adds recurrence, costly Hunts, and rare retirement but may disguise drafting as combat. Transformative resilience adds gate recovery and temporary benching but increases bookkeeping. The packet recommends testing earned curation against irreversible conquest, not accepting it outright. (source: [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

Consensus should accept the pillars, select lead/control models, resolve starting ownership, join recurrence with recovery, specify graft details using expected deck sizes, and address removal last against a demonstrated problem. (source: [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

## Confirmed design

Conquest-first acquisition, exact-kill recruitment/replacement, number-card targets, deck persistence, one primary card engine, recognizable rank/suit vocabulary, and the absence of fragments are accepted. Permanent removal is explicitly open—not confirmed and not forbidden. (sources: [[canon/v3/constraints|V3 constraints]], [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

## Current design work

Irreversible conquest, earned curation, and transformative resilience are coherent comparison models. Earned curation is the suggested lead for exploration, not the accepted answer. (source: [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

## Open questions

- What cards are owned at the start?
- Can missed enemies recur, and how deliberately?
- What recovery creates a comeback rather than automatic normalization?
- Is any permanent or temporary curation needed against a demonstrated problem?
