# Content bar

**Summary:** New content must change a visible decision and remain relevant under meaningful pressure.

Every content proposal faces three tests: does it change the player's choice, can the player attribute the result to it, and does it matter at campaign difficulty gates? Content that fails should be revised, combined, or removed. (source: [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]])

Items should own one legible rule bend with a cost. Offers should present incomparable shapes. Landmarks should have one recognizable verb and a route tradeoff. Fight rules must be visible before punishment and test plans without disabling a class by fiat. (source: [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]])

Simulation measures floors and outliers; playtesting judges ownership, readability, and fun. Numerical evidence cannot replace the content tests. (source: [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]])

For [[v3/mechanics/items-and-power-vehicles|items]], one legible rule bend should create a recognizable story and carry a cost, constraint, timing window, or opportunity cost. Flat micro-bonuses that duplicate a class or graft are doubly weak: they add vocabulary without changing a plan. Offers should therefore present incomparable shapes whose value changes with the deck, route, and upcoming pressure rather than a transparent numerical tier list. (source: [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]])

For [[v3/mechanics/forge-and-landmarks|landmarks]], one node should own one verb. Rest, Hunt, rearrangement, and relic tradeoff can each justify a route decision, but separate locations that all open interchangeable item menus create taxonomy without strategy. The older simulation's Forge/Market/Abbey result illustrates the failure mode even though its balance numbers are historical. (sources: [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]], [[research/simulation/findings|Simulation findings]])

Fight rules must be telegraphed before they punish, prefer mechanical pressure to small percentage changes, and test a plan without invalidating a class. This links the content bar to [[v3/stages/act-pressure-model|pressure packages]]: adaptation is valuable when it asks for a secondary line, not when an immunity simply turns off the chosen hero. (sources: [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]], [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

Measurement divides responsibilities. Simulation can find an outlier, dead option, or low floor; playtesting determines whether the player saw the cause, owned the decision, and enjoyed the consequence. A content review needs both forms because a numerically balanced feature can still be invisible or automatic. (source: [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]])
