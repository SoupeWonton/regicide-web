# Depth, width, and elegance

**Summary:** Good complexity comes from planning across turns with shared resources; bad complexity comes from pricing unrelated effects at one instant.

Depth is reasoning across time: sequencing a plan and living with resource consequences. Width is simultaneous comparison among incommensurable effects. V3 seeks difficult foresight while refusing spreadsheet-like resolution. (source: [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]])

The loop stations should compete through one shared currency—cards—across turns. Co-resolution is desirable when it creates a narratable sequence; it becomes a tax when several unrelated currencies fire automatically in one instant. (source: [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]])

A feature is elegant when it adds planning depth without proportionally adding width. The mastery test asks whether experience reveals deeper chains or merely automates repeated arithmetic. (source: [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]])

| Dimension | Depth worth preserving | Width worth removing |
|---|---|---|
| Currency | One shared pool, principally cards | Several effects with no common unit |
| Timespan | Consequences reasoned across turns | Everything priced during one atomic action |
| Choice | The player controls when and how to pay | Effects fire together regardless of intent |

The canonical good example is an exact-kill setup across two turns: choosing which cards to spend on blocking changes whether the exact-value answer survives for the next attack. Kill, block, and future setup interact, but the player can narrate the sequence through one scarce hand. The bad example asks one base-Regicide play to resolve damage, draw, recovery, shielding, immunity, and discard cost simultaneously. Both may be difficult; only the first difficulty becomes richer with foresight. (source: [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]])

Two tests govern new mechanics. The depth test asks whether difficulty comes from seeing far enough ahead or comparing too many unlike things now. The mastery test asks whether players discover longer chains or simply memorize arithmetic. These tests connect directly to [[v3/mechanics/items-and-power-vehicles|relic interaction]], [[v3/classes/class-progression-model|class deepening]], and [[v3/stages/act-pressure-model|Act 5 pressure]], where several interesting effects could easily become simultaneous bookkeeping. (source: [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]])

Elegance does not mean fewer features or trivial stations. Many features can coexist if they modify the existing loop, share its vocabulary, and express consequences across time. This correction matters because older migration prose described stations as individually trivial; current canon protects difficult, cross-turn competition while rejecting instant accounting. (sources: [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]], [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]])
