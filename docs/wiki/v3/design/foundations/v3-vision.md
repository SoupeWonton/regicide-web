# V3 vision

**Summary:** V3 turns deckbuilding into conquest: acquired cards come from the starting court or defeated enemies, while exact kills permanently reshape owned cards.

“You don't build a deck—you conquer one.” Permanent acquisition is tied to faced enemies rather than a generic draft or shop, and the familiar playing-card court remains the primary vocabulary. (source: [[canon/v3/vision|V3 vision]])

The deck is the main progression engine. Rank-or-suit replacement grafts deepen card identity without adding fragment wallets, spell inventories, or simultaneous suit arithmetic. (sources: [[canon/v3/vision|V3 vision]], [[canon/v3/constraints|V3 constraints]])

Permanent removal is neither accepted nor prohibited. It remains an open axis whose cost and effect on conquest identity require consensus before implementation. (sources: [[canon/v3/constraints|V3 constraints]], [[decisions/2026-06-20-design-practice-and-deck-curation|Deck curation decision]])

The phrase “conquer one” does several jobs at once. It ties reward to [[v3/mechanics/exact-kills|combat precision]], gives every recruit provenance, and turns [[v3/mechanics/persistent-expedition-deck|deck state]] into a record of the expedition. A generic card offer may be strategically serviceable, but it would weaken the story that this particular deck exists because of fights the player actually won. (sources: [[canon/v3/vision|V3 vision]], [[decisions/2026-06-18-v3-foundation|V3 foundation]])

The recognizable court is also a complexity boundary. Rank and suit already communicate power, combination, suit effects, immunities, and enemy hierarchy, so [[v3/mechanics/replacement-grafts|grafts]] deepen that familiar language rather than introducing an unlimited custom-card catalog. This makes card-face legibility a central product problem: transformation must remain visible enough that the court still feels recognizable. (sources: [[canon/v3/vision|V3 vision]], [[decisions/2026-06-18-v3-foundation|V3 foundation]])

The vision rejects parallel progression vehicles because they dilute both attention and fantasy. Fragments turn a meaningful kill into deferred currency; frequent spells and managed relic slots ask the player to maintain systems beside the deck. V3 instead lets rare [[v3/mechanics/items-and-power-vehicles|relics]] and [[v3/classes/class-identity|class loopholes]] reinterpret the card engine without replacing it. (sources: [[canon/v3/constraints|V3 constraints]], [[decisions/2026-06-18-v3-foundation|V3 foundation]])
