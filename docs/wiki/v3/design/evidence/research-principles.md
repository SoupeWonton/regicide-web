# Research principles

**Summary:** External deckbuilder research supplies evaluation questions about identity, connective play, synergy, randomness, accessibility, and presentation; it does not define Regicide rules.

The central thesis is that a deckbuilder needs a mechanically consequential identity, interesting choices inside and between fights, broad synergy surfaces, controlled randomness, legible progression, accessibility, and presentation that sustains slow play. (source: [[research/deckbuilding-roguelike-design-principles|Deckbuilding research]])

For Regicide, the strongest filter is whether ranks, suits, exact kills, the shared card economy, and campaign persistence create decisions that could only exist in campaign Regicide. Generic genre conventions should not displace conquest-first acquisition or the recognizable court. (source: [[research/deckbuilding-roguelike-design-principles|Deckbuilding research]])

The source was derived from a user-provided video transcript without title, author, URL, or date. Its recommendations are useful prompts but have incomplete provenance and remain research rather than canon. (source: [[research/deckbuilding-roguelike-design-principles|Deckbuilding research]])

The reference develops eight lenses: distinct mechanical identity; connective play between fights; category/rarity legibility; synergy and satisfaction over isolated balance; controlled randomness; deckbuilding as a progression loop rather than an interface; approachability; and presentation that sustains slow tactical play. (source: [[research/deckbuilding-roguelike-design-principles|Deckbuilding research]])

Applied to Regicide, those lenses ask whether the persistent shared deck changes routing and combat, whether landmarks create state-dependent tradeoffs, whether foundational pieces appear reliably, whether random possibilities can be manipulated, and whether players can see why a combination worked. They support [[v3/design/foundations/content-bar|content review]] but do not import another game's shop, rarity, or energy conventions by default. (source: [[research/deckbuilding-roguelike-design-principles|Deckbuilding research]])

The missing transcript attribution lowers provenance, not usefulness. Claims about other games should be treated as examples from the unknown source, while Regicide-specific invariants—seeded RNG, persistence, validation, quick-game isolation, and CT secrecy—remain grounded in this repository. (source: [[research/deckbuilding-roguelike-design-principles|Deckbuilding research]])
