# Lore and terminology

**Summary:** Current lore work defines mechanical pegs that need stories; the **2026-06-24 spine is now set** (God of Luck, five continents, crystals), but the broader world/narrative is not yet canon.

**Accepted spine (2026-06-24).** The player, wronged by a king, is recruited by the **God of Luck** and defeats successive kings across **five continents**. Between continents, the God of Luck runs a rigged top-of-deck **wager** (always lost). **Continent 4 loops** — the player has *become* the king, fighting versions of themselves (a dream/betrayal loop). The four spell **crystals**, when forged to **Full**, "emit a strange light"; completing all four ends the loop and opens the **Continent 5** God of Luck showdown. This spine is now authority; how each beat (Claim→Master) appears in-world is still to write. (source: [[decisions/2026-06-24-crystals-continents-and-equipment|Crystals decision]])

The active proposal asks for fiction around the Expedition, five continents, climaxes, Kingdom, meta unlocks, Camp, the 52-card deck, roadside landmarks, and hero identities. Its implied spine is heroes ascending through a realm to kill crowned royals. (source: [[proposals/lore/context-lore|Context lore proposal]])

The two-page PDF is an older snapshot using Provinces, three Jack–Queen–King acts, Gate/Courtyard/Throne, and Candle progression. Those structures predate the accepted five-act expedition and breadth-not-power progression, so the PDF is historical evidence rather than current direction. (sources: [[research/lore/context-lore.pdf|Older context lore PDF]], [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

The proposal inconsistently calls hero names both locked and placeholders. Final naming and broad lore remain deferred, while UI-critical terms should settle when required for comprehension. (sources: [[proposals/lore/context-lore|Context lore proposal]], [[proposals/later-design-backlog|Later backlog]])

The active pegs ask what an Expedition is, how Claim through Master appear in-world, what climaxes each act, whose Kingdom persists, what milestones represent, why Camps restore the deck, and whether the 52 cards express stamina, fate, allegiance, or another diegetic engine. Lair, Forge, Shrine, Tower, Market, Abbey, fights, and events are vignette slots rather than guaranteed mechanics. (source: [[proposals/lore/context-lore|Context lore proposal]])

Nine hero names exist as brainstorming placeholders, while only four roles are active V3 identities. Their motives and relationships remain open. Tone should support death as widening possibility and builds as personal stories, but mechanics must settle before broad narrative turns old structure into implied canon. (sources: [[proposals/lore/context-lore|Context lore proposal]], [[proposals/later-design-backlog|Later backlog]])
