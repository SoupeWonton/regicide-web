# V2 to V3 migration

**Summary:** V3 removes parallel economies and reframes the campaign around conquest, replacement, innate class identity, and **five continents** (Claim→Master) with an opt-in ending.

The migration replaces fragment rewards with immediate replacement grafts, removes the generic fragment shop, repurposes the Forge (now also **assembles spell crystals**), replaces token-only class identity with a starting hand + a **swappable Staff (passive enabler) × a kept ladder**, makes relics **rare equipment in four slots** (Staff is the fifth), turns spells into **fragment/half/full crystals**, and refuses gold. (sources: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]], [[decisions/2026-06-24-crystals-continents-and-equipment|2026-06-24 decision]])

It also reduces the active roster to four loop-defined classes, keeps the Ace as a low-rank companion, replaces the older campaign arc with a **five-continent** expedition (God of Luck overlay; finish by forging all four crystals to Full), and isolates campaign changes from quick-game Regicide. (source: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]])

The migration document's statement that each loop station is “individually trivial” is stale relative to the corrected elegance principle. Current canon permits difficult cross-turn competition among stations while rejecting simultaneous incommensurable arithmetic. (sources: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]], [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]])

The migration moves form clusters rather than independent tickets: graft semantics and generic-fragment-shop removal reshape card identity and rewards; the Staff×ladder classes and equipment-slot relics redefine power vehicles; the crystal-spell system and gold removal reshape economy width; the roster/Ace/rank bands shape acquisition; and five-continent continuity (with the opt-in ending) changes maps, saves, pacing, and fixtures. (source: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]])

Protected boundaries remain quick-game isolation, conquest-first entry, the continuous deck, deterministic campaign RNG, CT secrecy, and guarded save fields. The old token engine may be reused only where its behavior fits accepted replacement vocabulary; developed machinery does not preserve rejected semantics. (source: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]])

Sequencing starts with replacement and fragment-shop cleanup, then the crystal-spell system, class architecture (Staff×ladder), acquisition structure, equipment/relics, and the five-continent journey. Every slice must keep campaign mutations dispatched and persisted, validate before mutation, and preserve smoke coverage. (source: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]])
