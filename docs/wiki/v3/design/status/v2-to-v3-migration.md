# V2 to V3 migration

**Summary:** V3 removes parallel economies and reframes the campaign around conquest, replacement, innate class identity, and five continuous acts.

The migration replaces fragment rewards with immediate replacement grafts, removes the fragment shop, repurposes the Forge, replaces token-only class identity with a starting hand plus innate loophole, makes relics rare and slotless, folds spells into cards or actions, and refuses gold. (source: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]])

It also reduces the active roster to four loop-defined classes, keeps the Ace as a low-rank companion, replaces the older campaign arc with one five-act expedition, and isolates campaign changes from quick-game Regicide. (source: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]])

The migration document's statement that each loop station is “individually trivial” is stale relative to the corrected elegance principle. Current canon permits difficult cross-turn competition among stations while rejecting simultaneous incommensurable arithmetic. (sources: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]], [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]])

The thirteen migration moves form clusters rather than independent tickets: graft semantics and fragment removal reshape card identity and rewards; class loopholes and sparse relics redefine power vehicles; spell/gold removal collapses economy width; the roster/Ace/rank bands shape acquisition; and five-act continuity changes maps, saves, pacing, and fixtures. (source: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]])

Protected boundaries remain quick-game isolation, conquest-first entry, the continuous deck, deterministic campaign RNG, CT secrecy, and guarded save fields. The old token engine may be reused only where its behavior fits accepted replacement vocabulary; developed machinery does not preserve rejected semantics. (source: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]])

Sequencing starts with replacement and fragment cleanup, then spells, class architecture, acquisition structure, relics, and the five-act journey. Every slice must keep campaign mutations dispatched and persisted, validate before mutation, and preserve smoke coverage. (source: [[delivery/migration-v2-to-v3|Migration specification]])
