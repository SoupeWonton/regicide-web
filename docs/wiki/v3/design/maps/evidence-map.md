# Evidence map

**Summary:** Routes from research and playtest evidence to the design questions they can responsibly inform.

Simulation is strongest for relative comparisons, floors, outliers, and engine defects. Its absolute win rates are not forecasts, and its recorded datasets largely predate replacement grafts and the five-act expedition. (sources: [[research/simulation/findings|Simulation findings]], [[research/simulation/harness-notes|Harness notes]])

Raw playtests support claims about legibility, pacing, comeback stories, and emotional response. They mix direct tester comments with later interpretation, so the wiki must distinguish observation from inference. (sources: [[research/playtests/raw/When I overdraw, I should be able t.txt|Raw feedback]], [[research/playtests/raw/Updated_Raw.txt|Updated raw feedback]], [[archives/v2/playtests/raw/Final_Update.txt|Final raw feedback]])

Design assessments apply explicit rubrics to dated snapshots. The Golden Rules assessment is historical because it evaluates a slotted relic-tree model later rejected; the blast-radius assessment remains useful as a dependency map but contains at least one stale backfill assertion. (sources: [[research/assessments/golden-rules-2026-06-18|Golden Rules assessment]], [[research/assessments/canonical-drift-blast-radius|Blast radius]])

| Evidence | Strongest responsible use | Main limit |
|---|---|---|
| Simulation harness | Relative comparisons, outliers, engine faults, hypothesis generation | Old mechanics, limited maps, sub-human bots |
| Raw playtests | Legibility, pacing, emotional moments, voluntary behavior | Tiny sample; mixed direct speech and later interpretation |
| Golden Rules assessment | Tensions among synergy, discovery, and width | Scores an obsolete relic-tree snapshot |
| Blast-radius assessment | Dependency discovery and revalidation scope | Some assertions aged as canon changed |
| External design research | Evaluation questions and genre comparisons | Incomplete original provenance; not Regicide-specific evidence |

Evidence becomes useful when it is attached to a named hypothesis. The Tower result informs [[v3/mechanics/forge-and-landmarks|landmark purpose]], the comeback story informs [[v3/mechanics/persistent-expedition-deck|attrition and recovery]], and bot difficulty gaps inform [[v3/stages/act-pressure-model|pressure validation]]. None of them independently selects the final design because [[v3/design/foundations/design-authority-and-states|consensus]] remains the transition from evidence to intended behavior. (sources: [[research/simulation/findings|Simulation findings]], [[research/playtests/raw/Updated_Raw.txt|Updated raw feedback]], [[canon/principles/design-practice|Design practice]])
