# V3 system map

**Summary:** A compact relationship map for the accepted V3 direction.

The [[v3/design/foundations/v3-vision|V3 vision]] makes conquest the source of deck growth. Combat expresses that vision through the [[v3/mechanics/core-loop|core loop]] and [[v3/mechanics/exact-kills|exact kills]], producing either recruitment or [[v3/mechanics/replacement-grafts|replacement grafts]]. (sources: [[canon/v3/vision|V3 vision]], [[canon/v3/core-loop|Core loop]])

The [[v3/mechanics/persistent-expedition-deck|persistent expedition deck]] carries those consequences through the [[v3/stages/five-act-expedition|five-act expedition]]. The first two acts acquire and shape the deck; the last three test exploitation, adaptation, and mastery. (sources: [[canon/v3/constraints|V3 constraints]], [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

Each class owns one station of the shared loop: [[v3/classes/sentinel|Sentinel]] blocks, [[v3/classes/executioner|Executioner]] kills, [[v3/classes/quartermaster|Quartermaster]] combines, and [[v3/classes/surgeon|Surgeon]] persists. Detailed kits and progression remain unresolved. (source: [[canon/v3/classes/overview|Classes]])

Rare relics and route landmarks support the card engine without becoming parallel inventories or currencies. [[v3/mechanics/items-and-power-vehicles|Items]] and [[v3/mechanics/forge-and-landmarks|landmarks]] remain structurally bounded but need detailed design. (sources: [[canon/v3/systems/items|Items]], [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]])

The system is best read as one causal chain. [[v3/mechanics/conquest-first-acquisition|Conquest]] determines which cards enter; [[v3/mechanics/exact-kills|precision]] decides whether an encounter changes the deck; [[v3/mechanics/replacement-grafts|replacement]] gives owned enemies continued value; and [[v3/mechanics/persistent-expedition-deck|persistence]] makes those outcomes matter beyond one fight. The campaign then applies pressure to the accumulated history rather than issuing a fresh deck at each act. (sources: [[canon/v3/vision|V3 vision]], [[canon/v3/constraints|V3 constraints]], [[canon/v3/campaign/run-and-progression|Run and progression]])

Three complexity budgets keep that chain legible. [[v3/design/foundations/depth-width-and-elegance|Elegance]] keeps difficulty in cross-turn planning; [[v3/design/foundations/content-bar|the content bar]] rejects rewards or locations that do not change visible choices; and [[v3/design/foundations/golden-rules|the Golden Rules]] demand tradeoffs, divergence, discovery, and memorable contextual combinations. Classes, relics, landmarks, and pressure packages must therefore enrich the same card engine instead of competing with it for attention. (sources: [[canon/principles/depth-width-elegance|Depth vs. width]], [[canon/principles/content-bar|Content bar]], [[canon/principles/golden-rules|Golden rules]])

The unresolved work clusters where one choice changes several downstream systems: [[v3/mechanics/deck-lifecycle|deck lifecycle]] controls deck size and recovery; [[v3/classes/class-progression-model|class progression]] controls identity breadth; [[v3/stages/act-pressure-model|pressure design]] determines what those builds must prove; and [[v3/design/product/ui-ux-contract|UI]] must make transformed identity and consequences trustworthy. (source: [[proposals/open-design-questions|Active design questions]])
