# Evidence limitations

**Summary:** Research informs decisions but must be dated, scoped, and checked against newer canon before reuse.

The Golden Rules assessment is a historical snapshot built around a slotted, expanding relic-tree model that conflicts with the accepted rare, slotless relic direction. Its rubric and tensions remain informative; its target design and numerical scores are not current specifications. (sources: [[research/assessments/golden-rules-2026-06-18|Golden Rules assessment]], [[canon/v3/systems/items|Items]])

The blast-radius assessment is valuable for dependency clusters but states that canon has “no backfill” while current canon allows backfill for pacing and the active lifecycle packet treats recurrence and recovery as unresolved. Its backfill row must be read as stale assessment text. (sources: [[research/assessments/canonical-drift-blast-radius|Blast radius]], [[canon/v3/systems/deck-and-grafts|Deck and grafts]], [[proposals/systems/deck-lifecycle|Deck lifecycle packet]])

The lore PDF predates the five-act expedition, simulation data predates foundational mechanic changes, and raw playtests mix direct observations with LLM interpretation. None should silently override newer canon or substitute for new validation after radius-four or radius-five changes. (sources: [[research/lore/context-lore.pdf|Older lore PDF]], [[research/simulation/harness-notes|Harness notes]], [[archives/v2/playtests/raw/Final_Update.txt|Final raw feedback]])

| Source | May support | Must not establish |
|---|---|---|
| Golden Rules assessment | Design tensions and historical scoring rationale | Current relic architecture or authoritative scores |
| Blast-radius assessment | Dependencies and revalidation scope | Its stale no-backfill wording |
| Simulation datasets | Relative hypotheses and engine/bot defects | Current V3 win rates or final balance |
| Raw playtests | Observed confusion, excitement, and behavior | Broad player preference or LLM interpretations as testimony |
| Lore PDF | Historical terminology and narrative lineage | Five-act or breadth-based current lore |
| External research | Evaluation questions | Regicide requirements without consensus |

Evidence has an expiry condition: a source remains useful until a changed mechanic breaks the causal assumptions behind it. Replacement grafts invalidate additive-card balance; a new lifecycle changes acquisition floors; class facets change pressure interaction; five acts change pacing and retention. The correct response is to retain provenance while commissioning new validation, not delete inconvenient history or quote it as timeless fact. (sources: [[research/assessments/canonical-drift-blast-radius|Blast radius]], [[research/assessments/README|Assessments]])
