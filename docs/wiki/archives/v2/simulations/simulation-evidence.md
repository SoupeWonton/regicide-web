# Simulation evidence

**Summary:** Seeded bots reveal relative differences, outliers, engine faults, and strategic floors, but the recorded datasets describe an older campaign and bot policy.

The harness drives real engines with six weighted personas. Relative comparisons on paired seeds are considered more reliable than absolute win rates because bots omit multi-turn coordination, information valuation, and strong royal planning. (sources: [[research/simulation/harness-notes|Harness notes]], [[research/simulation/findings|Simulation findings]])

Recorded findings include a much stronger Camp than Lair outcome, a solo Tower with no functional reward, statistically similar Forge/Market/Abbey menus, costly overkill behavior, severe gate losses, and a Quartermaster effect that appeared especially load-bearing in older class experiments. (sources: [[research/simulation/findings|Simulation findings]], [[research/simulation/harness-notes|Harness notes]])

Those results predate current replacement grafts, the final deck lifecycle, current class exploration, and the five-act expedition. They support hypotheses and regression questions but cannot establish current V3 balance. (sources: [[research/simulation/findings|Simulation findings]], [[research/assessments/canonical-drift-blast-radius|Blast radius]])

Additional strategy notes identify older base-game interactions around solo immunity, matched low-card combinations, hand-size pressure, Jester sequencing, recruited royals, and the Hearts-to-Diamonds recovery pipeline. These are useful engine hypotheses, but several depend on old royal recruitment or quick-game rules rather than accepted V3 campaign behavior. (source: [[research/strategy/additional-strategy|Additional strategy]])

Findings are graded **CLEAR** when robust to bot quality, **INFERRED** when policy may create the number, and **DISCUSS** when data cannot choose the design. Clear older signals included Camp outperforming Lair, a no-op solo Tower, interchangeable item menus, and measurable cost to overkill. Item weakness, event generosity, and precision value remained bot-sensitive. (source: [[research/simulation/findings|Simulation findings]])

The harness spans campaign and base-game datasets with aggression, defense, economy, precision, control, and mixed personas. Known limitations include sub-human boss planning, underpriced information, limited map coverage, base-game random variance, and old class/rule assumptions. Relative paired-seed comparisons are therefore stronger than win-rate forecasts. (sources: [[research/simulation/harness-notes|Harness notes]], [[research/simulation/findings|Simulation findings]])

Revalidation is mandatory after radius-five card identity, lifecycle, class, or campaign changes. Old results remain useful for experimental design—especially route comparisons and bot failure modes—but not for tuning current V3. (source: [[research/assessments/canonical-drift-blast-radius|Blast radius]])
