# Final Update playtest note

**Summary:** This historical V2 note preserves an LLM-assisted interpretation of a collaborator voluntarily playing two runs, reporting unqualified fun, and shifting attention toward presentation work.

## What the source contains

The source transcribes a short French conversation and then includes an LLM's interpretation. The collaborator reports having local visual work, intends to make that work the branch baseline, says he completed two runs while impaired by cannabis, states that he is having fun, and immediately asks for the game to look better. (source: [[research/playtests/raw/Final_Update.txt|Final Update source]])

The later analysis interprets the sequence as movement from criticism through engagement and retention toward ownership. That framing is an interpretation supplied by the source's chatbot response, not an independently measured player-state model. (source: [[research/playtests/raw/Final_Update.txt|Final Update source]])

## Why it mattered in V2

The note was treated as a positive qualitative signal because the play occurred voluntarily during unrelated development work and the complaint shifted from questioning the loop toward improving presentation. It does not establish balance, broad-market retention, or V3 quality; it records one collaborator's behavior in a V2-shaped build. (source: [[research/playtests/raw/Final_Update.txt|Final Update source]])

## Evidence limits

- One collaborator is not representative external testing.
- The source combines literal transcription with enthusiastic LLM interpretation.
- Substance impairment makes the session unusual rather than controlled.
- The build predates the V3 mechanical and campaign decisions.
- Presentation investment may indicate ownership without validating system balance. (source: [[research/playtests/raw/Final_Update.txt|Final Update source]])
