# Playtest signals

**Summary:** Raw feedback suggests early-pressure and legibility problems alongside strong comeback stories and voluntary replay, but later interpretations must not be confused with direct testimony.

Direct observations reported missing hand context during overdraw, occasional trigger failures, a useless Tower, broken or unclear token effects, easy early fights followed by a boss spike, unclear class labels, and a broken Forge. These refer to an older developed build. (source: [[research/playtests/raw/When I overdraw, I should be able t.txt|Raw feedback]])

The strongest positive direct signal was a narrated recovery from an empty hand through a chain of relic, draw, Ace, block, and Jester interactions. The tester later reported having fun, played two runs voluntarily, and invested effort in presentation. (sources: [[research/playtests/raw/Updated_Raw.txt|Updated raw feedback]], [[archives/v2/playtests/raw/Final_Update.txt|Final raw feedback]])

The raw files include extensive LLM interpretation about engagement, retention, ownership, and founder behavior. Those readings are hypotheses, not verbatim tester evidence, and need human confirmation before guiding design. (sources: [[research/playtests/raw/Updated_Raw.txt|Updated raw feedback]], [[archives/v2/playtests/raw/Final_Update.txt|Final raw feedback]])

The evidence has a timeline. Early direct notes identify overdraw visibility, inconsistent triggers, unclear labels, broken effects, low opening pressure, and a boss spike. A later direct account narrates a recovery chain from zero cards; the final exchange reports fun, two voluntary runs, and a desire to improve presentation. (sources: [[research/playtests/raw/When I overdraw, I should be able t.txt|Raw feedback]], [[research/playtests/raw/Updated_Raw.txt|Updated feedback]], [[archives/v2/playtests/raw/Final_Update.txt|Final feedback]])

Later prose interprets that sequence as criticism → engagement → retention → ownership. **Inference:** this is a plausible design reading, but it is produced by an LLM and should not be treated as the tester's own causal explanation. The durable signal is narrower: pressure timing and legibility need work, while comeback sequences can create memorable stories. (sources: [[research/playtests/raw/Updated_Raw.txt|Updated feedback]], [[archives/v2/playtests/raw/Final_Update.txt|Final feedback]])

Because the build used old tokens, relics, Provinces, and royal behavior, the feedback cannot validate replacement grafts or five-act pacing. It can still guide hypotheses for [[v3/design/product/ui-ux-contract|visibility]], [[v3/stages/act-pressure-model|pressure onset]], and [[v3/design/foundations/golden-rules|story-producing chains]]. (sources: [[research/playtests/raw/When I overdraw, I should be able t.txt|Raw feedback]], [[research/playtests/raw/Updated_Raw.txt|Updated feedback]])
