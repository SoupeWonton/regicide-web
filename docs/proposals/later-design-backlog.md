# Later design backlog

These topics are intentionally outside the current decision horizon. They are not
rejected and do not belong in the active question queue until their dependencies are
stable.

## Additional classes and detailed meta unlocks

Do not design the five parked V2 classes until the four core identities are legible,
deep, and fun. Meta progression is accepted to widen options rather than provide
required permanent statistics; exact class/faction unlocks, facet triggers, relic
packages, routes, shortcuts, and permutations remain later work.

Current breadth hypothesis:
[`classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations.md`](classes/facets-and-pressure-permutations.md).

## Detailed relic expansion

Do not build a large relic catalog until graft, class, and landmark roles are stable.
New relics must create demonstrated variation without becoming spell inventory or class
progression under another name.

## Long-run balance and high difficulty

Do not preserve detailed balance conclusions across replacement grafts, the final deck
lifecycle, new class kits, and five-continent pressure content. Rebaseline once the alpha
feature set stabilizes.

## Lore and final naming

UI-critical terms must settle when required for comprehension. Broader narrative,
faction lore, and final naming can follow the stable mechanical structure.
