# Class progression — facet & linear candidate pool (for elimination)

**Status:** Brainstorm for a Landry↔Gab elimination session. **Deliberately over-generated**
— more options than will survive, so the cut is informed. Names and numbers are
**illustrative placeholders**; the point is the *shape and feel* of each option, not balance.

Feeds **Q5** ([open questions](../open-design-questions.md)). The two competing structures:
[`four-core-classes.md`](four-core-classes.md) (linear) vs
[`facets-and-pressure-permutations.md`](facets-and-pressure-permutations.md) (facets).

## The distinction, made concrete (Sentinel)

- **Linear** = the class *is* its progression. Sentinel always climbs one fixed ladder;
  two Sentinel runs differ only by deck/relics/route. *Simpler to build/teach; risk: same-y replays.*
- **Facet** = the class is a *verb*; you pick one **mutually-exclusive dialect** mid-run
  (~Act 2, after seeing your deck). Same Block loophole, genuinely different characters;
  meta unlocks add facets to the pool (breadth without new classes). *Richer; ~3× content to balance/teach.*

> **Philosophical note:** facets are the class-layer version of "**breadth via choice, not
> vertical power**" — the same principle behind the 4-slot spell tiers and unique relics.
> If those are accepted, facets rhyme with them; linear is a slight mismatch.

## How to use this doc

For each class: pick **at most one** linear ladder **or** a facet set (2–3 to ship +
1–2 unlockable). Mark **KEEP / CUT / MAYBE** in the worksheet at the bottom. Flag 🪜 = a
floor-relevant option (eases card-starvation), since that's an open pain point.

## SENTINEL — Block *(defense / inevitability)*
*Loophole: blocking pays instead of taxing.*

### Linear ladders (pick one)
- **L1 · The Wall** *(canonical):* Aegis (block to 0 → draw 1) → Bulwark (surplus shield →
  bonus dmg + Fortify bank) → Impenetrable (1×/fight negate **and** reflect).
  *Why:* cleanest inevitability fantasy. *Scales:* each tier adds a payoff for over-blocking
  until blocking is a win condition.
- **L2 · The Tide** *(defense→burst):* Backstop (excess shield carries to next turn) →
  Undertow (stored shield convertible to ♣ on demand) → Seawall (release the whole bank as
  one huge attack). *Why:* gives the slow game a burst finish. *Scales:* your defense *is*
  your offense reservoir — grows with patience.
- **L3 · The Vigil** *(defense→card economy)* 🪜: Watch (each block draws toward a cap) →
  Ward (blocking protects a chosen card from discard/attrition) → Eternal Vigil (hand never
  starts below N). *Why:* ties Block to anti-starvation. *Scales:* defense feeds the deck engine.

### Facets (pick a set)
- **Bastion** — bank surplus shield; spend to pre-empt a gate's alpha strike. *Patient,
  plans across acts; wins gates by pre-loading.*
- **Riposte** — exact block → next attack +blocked value, opening exact-kill windows.
  *Aggressive-off-defense; an exact-kill hunter in armor.*
- **Protector** 🪜 — exact block returns the Spade to hand / shields a key card. *An
  unkillable engine that never loses its tools.*
- **Reflector** — block to 0 reflects part of the hit as damage. *Passive chip damage; low-effort, attritional.*
- **Provoker** — bait a harder counterattack, then profit more from blocking it. *Proactive risk/punish.*

## EXECUTIONER — Kill *(aggression / snowball)*
*Loophole: the exact-kill window widens; kills cascade.*

### Linear ladders (pick one)
- **L1 · Snowball** *(canonical):* Open Season (window widens to 1–3 on the road) → Kill
  Chain (each exact kill draws; consecutive kills widen further) → Reign of Terror (recruited
  royals hit harder; kills cascade into draws/damage). *Why:* pure momentum. *Scales:*
  compounds faster than enemy pressure.
- **L2 · Headsman** *(precision over volume):* Clean Cut (exact kill refunds a card) →
  Measured Strike (preview exact thresholds; reliability) → Decapitation (one guaranteed
  exact per gate). *Why:* rewards finesse, lowers variance. *Scales:* exact kills get
  *cheaper/surer*, not wider — contrasts L1's greed.
- **L3 · Warlord** *(recruit-focused):* Conscript (exact-kill recruits some normally-banished
  cards) → Press-gang (recruits enter readier) → Horde (deck grows faster and bigger).
  *Why:* leans into conquest-as-acquisition. *Scales:* deck size/power growth.

### Facets (pick a set)
- **Momentum** — one exact kill accelerates the next (draw + widen the chain). *Combo-chain tempo.*
- **Bounty** — exact kills reveal/improve future targets and duplicate-graft choices. *Sculpts the conquered deck.*
- **Kingslayer** — road kills bank "execution" charge; unleash on royals/gates. *Save-up-for-the-boss burst.*
- **Reaper** 🪜 — overkill is no longer wasted (partial value carries, or recruits anyway).
  *Forgiving aggression — lowers the exact-kill skill floor.*
- **Duelist** — bonuses for finishing an enemy in one turn / few cards. *High-tempo blitz; speed over chain.*

## QUARTERMASTER — Combine *(tempo / big-turn)*
*Loophole: hand-cap and combo-cap are broken.*

### Linear ladders (pick one)
- **L1 · Overwhelm** *(canonical):* Requisition (hand +2; first ♦ draws extra) → Arsenal
  (♦ draws more; combos up to 12) → Last Requisition (empty hand → refill full, repeatable).
  *Why:* explosive single turns. *Scales:* each tier makes the big turn bigger.
- **L2 · Logistics** *(efficiency over size):* Supply Line (cheaper combos / Ace
  flexibility) → Depot (hold cards across encounters without discard) → Grand Logistics
  (reconfigure a combo before commit). *Why:* cerebral optionality. *Scales:* flexibility,
  not raw size — contrasts L1's bigness.
- **L3 · Mobilize** *(draw engine)* 🪜: Muster (every ♦ overdraws more) → Reserves (keep more
  from the overdraw pool) → Total Mobilization (draw the deck thin, then refill). *Why:*
  leans into ♦/overdraw identity; anti-starvation. *Scales:* card-flow volume.

### Facets (pick a set)
- **Arsenal** — raise the combo ceiling; widen legal compositions; one decisive turn. *Build-the-bomb.*
- **Reserve** — hold the right answer for later; recover it for a gate phase. *Planner/toolbox.*
- **Logistics** — Ace flexibility / recompose combos before commit. *Combinatorial puzzle.*
- **Tithe** 🪜 — overdraw bigger, then convert the unkept remainder into shield/value.
  *Turns excess cards into survivability.*
- **Blitz** — big turns also draw, chaining multiple big turns. *Explosive recursion.*

## SURGEON — Persist *(recursion / value)*
*Loophole: the Tavern never dries; the best cards return.*

### Linear ladders (pick one)
- **L1 · Engine** *(canonical):* Triage (♥ fires every Heart, recycling discard) → Flush
  System (recover more; choose high-value/recruited cards) → Full Capacity (Tavern dry →
  return ALL discards, repeatable). *Why:* the deck never dies. *Scales:* recursion volume.
- **L2 · Field Medic** *(selective quality):* Stabilize (recover a specific transformed
  card) → Operate (return it ready / closer to play) → Miracle (one full recovery from
  collapse). *Why:* surgical precision. *Scales:* targeting, not volume — contrasts L1.
- **L3 · Plague Doctor** *(attrition-flip):* Inoculate (recovered cards return tokened/
  stronger) → Contagion (recovery also pressures the enemy) → Pandemic (the long game tips
  to you). *Why:* aggressive read of persist. *Scales:* recursion becomes active advantage.

### Facets (pick a set)
- **Triage** — recover *specific* cards (quality). *Curate the loop.*
- **Circulation** 🪜 — keep Tavern/discard moving continuously (throughput). *Never stalls — strongest anti-starvation engine.*
- **Resurrection** — emergency comeback from near-collapse. *Brink-survivor.*
- **Transfusion** 🪜 — recovery shares value (heal hand size / convert recover→draw). *♥ doubles as ♦; cross-axis flex.*
- **Embalmer** — recovered recruited/royal cards return permanently stronger. *Snowball via recursion.*

## Cross-cutting observations (to weigh during the cut)

- **Floor coverage:** every class has at least one 🪜 option (Vigil/Protector, Reaper,
  Mobilize/Tithe, Circulation/Transfusion). If the floor fix lands at the *class* layer
  instead of a global rule, prefer keeping one 🪜 per class.
- **Facets multiply, linears don't.** 4 classes × 3 facets × 2 hands × 5 pressure
  permutations ≈ 120 macro-configs (per the facets packet) before deck/relics/seed. Linear
  gives 4 fixed identities — replay variety must then come entirely from deck/relics/route.
- **Teaching/balance cost:** linear = 12 tier-effects total to author/balance; facets =
  ~12 facets × partial progression each, plus the mid-run choice UI and the "facet legible
  without the name" test (guardrail #9 in the facets packet).
- **Hybrid worth a look:** a *fixed loophole tier-1* (so the class reads instantly) **+** a
  facet chosen at Act 2 that supplies tiers 2–3. Captures linear's legibility-at-second-zero
  and facets' divergence. Not yet written up — flag if the table below leaves you wanting it.
