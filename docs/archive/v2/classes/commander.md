# Commander

## Class Identity

Tier: 2 — Support Hero  
Theme: Initiative  
Guiding question: Who should act next?  
Unlock condition: unlocked after first chapter completion or on replacement after a Tier 1 hero death.

The Commander is the initiative-control specialist. It reduces turn-order disasters without removing encounter tension.

## Expedition Stability Profile

Primary failure mode addressed: wrong player owns initiative after a kill

## Core Ability

Once per enemy, after any kill, Commander may transfer initiative: the active player passes the next turn to any ally instead of playing again.

### What it actually does

- mitigates forced consecutive-turn exposure
- hands key transitions to the best-positioned hero
- improves reliability in boss and elite kill transitions

### Timing window

Best use cases:
- killer has weak follow-up hand
- another hero has exact setup for next enemy
- phase transitions where one wrong follow-up can cause collapse

Worst use cases:
- all hands are equally strong
- low-risk enemies where redirecting provides no strategic value

## Specialization Paths

## Vanguard — Turn Mastery

Once per chapter, completely ignore the consecutive-turn rule after any kill. The team may assign the next turn freely.

### Effect profile

- high impact at one critical transition point
- strongest in boss phases and elite pressure windows
- should be held for phase-defining moments, not convenience

## Signal Officer — Preparation Economy

Once per camp, one preparation activation is treated as discounted or upgraded according to chapter economy rules.

### Effect profile

- converts initiative class value into planning-layer leverage
- strongest when preparation pools are deep and camp choices are meaningful
- weaker in low-preparation chapters

## Strategic Interactions

Strong teammates:
- Sentinel: safer handoff into mitigation turns
- Executioner: cleaner exact-kill sequencing into planned follow-up
- Quartermaster: better chance the designated next hero has a functional hand

Risk profile:
- lower value in teams with little role differentiation
- can be misplayed by spending Vanguard too early

## Common Misplays

- using Vanguard on non-critical transitions
- redirecting turns without checking next-turn hand quality
- choosing Signal Officer in chapters with weak preparation options

## Build Guidance

Pick Vanguard if:
- your failures cluster around kill-transition moments
- your team has role-specialized hands that punish wrong turn ownership

Pick Signal Officer if:
- camp preparation planning is the main bottleneck
- your team already handles transitions reasonably well

## Difficulty Impact

Compared to base game:
- directly addresses a core Regicide structural failure point
- lowers positional failure frequency without deleting pressure
- remains strong but should sit near ~1.0 CT, not as a dominant auto-pick
