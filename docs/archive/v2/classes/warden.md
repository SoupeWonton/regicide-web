# Warden

## Class Identity

Tier: 2 — Support Hero
Theme: Crisis Management
Guiding question: How do we survive catastrophe?
Unlock condition: unlocked after first chapter completion or on replacement after a Tier 1 hero death.

The Warden is the anti-collapse hero. It specializes in death recovery, retreat decisions, last stand execution, and emergency preparation.

A party loses a hero and recruits a Warden. The feeling is not:

> We are weaker now.

It is:

> We have someone who knows how to keep an expedition alive.

## Expedition Stability Profile

| Pillar | Rating | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Shield | 0 | — |
| Access | 0 | — |
| Recovery | 2 | crisis recovery and death fork expansion |
| Initiative | 1 | death fork reshapes turn structure |
| Consistency | 0 | — |

CT per chapter: ~1.0 to ~1.25
Specialization bonus: Iron Last Stand +0.75 / Battle Retreat +0.25 to +0.5
Note: Warden remains a high-leverage crisis class, but should not be the automatic best replacement in all compositions
Primary failure mode addressed: all five failure modes — Warden is a systemic safety net

## Core Ability

Once per expedition run, when entering a death fork, the Warden adds one additional choice to the available fork options.

### What it actually does

- expands the decision space at the most critical narrative moment
- lets the party access an option that might otherwise not be possible
- strongest when the available fork choices are all bad without an alternative

### Timing window

Best use cases:
- when the standard three death-fork options all lead to likely collapse
- when a specific recovery path is unavailable without the extra choice
- in boss phases where death-fork options are extremely constrained

Worst use cases:
- saving it for a death that never comes
- using it on a fork where all existing options are already good

## Specialization Paths

## Iron Last Stand — Amplified Survival

When the party chooses Last Stand after a hero death, the next player draws up to their hand cap immediately before continuing.

### Effect profile

- directly addresses the weakest moment of Last Stand: the next player's hand is exhausted
- restores a degree of capability to the surviving team before they must face the enemy
- strongest in long boss fights where the death happens mid-phase

### Interactions

- Quartermaster Logistician: refill plus filter immediately after the draw.
- Sentinel: if the draw finds Spades, shield infrastructure can be rebuilt quickly.
- Surgeon Physician: both prevent death outcomes. In Last Stand, Physician can keep a hero alive with minimal hand; Iron Last Stand then restores that hand.

### Best play pattern

Road:
- lean into Last Stand as a viable option rather than a last resort.
- route toward encounters where enemy state allows a surviving team to finish.

Camp:
- pre-assign which hero would take the death-trigger role in extremis.

Boss:
- hold Iron Last Stand for phase-transition deaths, not early incidental kills.

## Battle Retreat — Rewarded Withdrawal

When the party chooses Retreat after a hero death, the team keeps the full route reward instead of partial currency. The penalty is a longer road to camp.

### Effect profile

- removes the economic sting of Retreat
- lets teams treat Retreat as a strategic choice rather than a pure loss
- strongest in chapters where route rewards are highly valuable

### Interactions

- Merchant Quartermaster: reward preservation plus extra prep slot equals strong camp economy.
- Hunter Executioner: kept rewards fund the follow-up boss preparation chain.
- Kingdom progression: faster currency accumulation across a campaign of retreats.

### Best play pattern

Road:
- Retreat is now a valid tactical option; plan routes around it rather than avoiding it.
- identify encounters where a retreat-and-restart produces better overall chapter outcomes.

Camp:
- spend the preserved reward immediately to reduce vulnerability before the next encounter.

Boss:
- if a death-fork happens in boss phase, Battle Retreat is less applicable; use the extra fork option from core ability instead.

## Strategic Interactions

Suit interactions:
- Hearts: Warden synergizes with recovery timing; refilled hands after Last Stand often come from Hearts cycles.
- Diamonds: draw after Last Stand is particularly strong when Diamonds are available immediately.

Campaign engine interactions:
- Death fork: Warden transforms the death fork from a crisis into a decision with real options.
- Narrative arc: Warden is the strongest class for preserving memory-bearing heroes across chapters.
- Chapter continuity: Battle Retreat supports a campaign arc of many partial runs building toward a final clear.

## Best Team Context

Best alongside:
- Surgeon for layered death prevention
- Quartermaster for economic recovery after retreats
- any memory-bearing Tier 1 hero the party cannot afford to lose

Risky alongside:
- all-in aggressive parties that never plan for death
- teams where the best outcome is always Last Stand and Battle Retreat has no application

## Common Misplays

- holding core ability indefinitely and never using it
- choosing Iron Last Stand in parties with no draw support
- using Battle Retreat when the route reward is irrelevant to the chapter plan

## Build Guidance

Pick Iron Last Stand if:
- party plays aggressively and deaths happen mid-combat often
- team has draw support to make the immediate refill meaningful

Pick Battle Retreat if:
- campaign prioritizes economic continuity and kingdom progression speed
- the team frequently reaches high-pressure encounters in weakened states

## Difficulty Impact

Compared to base game:
- directly addresses the emotional severity of loss
- preserves the Darkest Dungeon tone by making collapse survivable rather than painless
- death still costs something; Warden shapes the cost instead of removing it
