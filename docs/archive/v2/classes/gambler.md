# Gambler

## Class Identity

Tier: 3 — Weird Hero
Theme: Uncertainty
Guiding question: Can we turn disaster into opportunity?
Interaction layer: probability, risk, and meta systems

Gambler is controlled volatility. It trades certainty for initiative control and explosive momentum shifts.

## Expedition Stability Profile

| Pillar | Rating | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Shield | 0 | — |
| Access | 0 | — |
| Recovery | 0 | — |
| Initiative | 2 | wager redirects turn ownership on success |
| Consistency | 1 | Jester manipulation reshapes probability |

CT per chapter: variable (~0.5 to ~1.5 depending on player discipline)
Specialization bonus: Cardsharp variable (avg ~0) / Fate Broker +0.25
Note: Gambler does not smooth variance — it bets on it. In skilled hands it increases CT significantly. In undisciplined hands it reduces it.
Primary failure mode addressed: wrong player owns initiative after a kill

Core ability:
- Once per chapter before resolving a play, declare a wager:
  - Success (enemy dies this turn): choose next player.
  - Failure: active player discards one random card after attack resolution.

Specializations:
- Cardsharp: gain two wagers per chapter, but each failed wager applies +1 discard-needed.
- Fate Broker: when a Jester is played, may redraw one card for two different players.

## What Gambler Actually Does

Gambler is a tempo manipulation class disguised as a risk class.

It changes:
- who takes important turns,
- how often a party can chain optimal hands,
- whether the team can skip bad consecutive-turn outcomes.

It is strongest in coordinated groups that can estimate kill probability accurately.

## Core Ability Deep Analysis

### Wager quality

A good wager is not just "likely kill." It is "kill that changes turn ownership value."

Best wager moments:
- enemy death hands turn to fragile player by default and must be redirected
- boss transition turns where next actor matters more than current damage
- post-camp turns where one hero is fully primed

Bad wager moments:
- uncertain thresholds with high random-card-discard downside
- states where initiative selection provides no meaningful edge

### Interaction map

Strong interactions:
- Executioner (cleaner kill certainty).
- Commander (initiative and turn control).
- Quartermaster (better hand quality increases wager confidence).

Risk amplification:
- Exile and Oracle builds with high variance can over-punish failed bets.

## Specialization Path: Cardsharp

Text:
- Gain two wagers per chapter, but each failed wager applies +1 discard-needed.

### What it does strategically

Cardsharp is high-ceiling, high-punishment tempo abuse. It can dominate chapters if successful, but failed bets compound lethal pressure quickly.

Use Cardsharp when:
- team has strong threshold prediction (Executioner/Oracle support),
- chapter rewards initiative control heavily,
- players are disciplined about pass opportunities.

### Interactions

Best with:
- Hunter Executioner (higher kill precision).
- Sentinel (buffers failure cost).

Risk:
- repeated failure causes cascading discard collapses.

Best play pattern:
1. Reserve first wager for high-certainty tactical swing.
2. Only use second wager if state remains stable.
3. If one wager fails, shift to conservative lines immediately.

## Specialization Path: Fate Broker

Text:
- When a Jester is played, may redraw one card for two different players.

### What it does strategically

Fate Broker turns Jester from pure emergency tool into a team hand-quality reset.

Use Fate Broker when:
- chapter includes many reactive crises,
- team relies on Jester timing to recover structure,
- party has low baseline consistency.

### Interactions

Best with:
- Quartermaster Logistician (compound filtering).
- Guardian Sentinel (net-0 plus redraw quality loops).

Risk:
- if Jester access is rare, value can be inconsistent.

Best play pattern:
1. Play Jester when two players are hand-locked.
2. Use redraw to restore role-critical function (shield/draw/healing).
3. Spend regained initiative to reposition turn ownership.

## Best Way to Play Gambler

Road:
- wager only when initiative gain has high strategic value.

Camp:
- decide specialization based on chapter volatility and Jester density.

Boss:
- save wager for phase-turn where next actor determines survival.

## Common Misplays

- wagering for style kills with no initiative consequence.
- taking Cardsharp without mitigation support.
- using Fate Broker in low-Jester ecosystems.

## Build Guidance

Pick Cardsharp if:
- team is precise and disciplined.
- goal is high-tempo chapter clear.

Pick Fate Broker if:
- team is volatile and needs recovery flexibility.
- campaign has frequent Jester-dependent turns.

## Difficulty Impact

Compared to base game:
- raises ceiling and raises fail-state risk.
- can feel empowering without removing scarcity tension.
