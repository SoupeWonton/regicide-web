# Sentinel

## Identity

Tier: 1 — Core Hero
Theme: Shield / Stability
Suit: Spades
Guiding question: How do we survive tomorrow?

The Sentinel is the party's damage governor. This class converts Spade timing into survival margin and is strongest when the table plans around incoming discard thresholds, not around damage races.

## Expedition Stability Profile

| Pillar | Rating | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Shield | 3 | primary function |

Core role:
- flatten attack spikes
- protect fragile hands before boss transitions
- enable safer kill chains by lowering counterattack risk

## Core Ability

Once per enemy, one Spade gains +2 shield value.

### What it actually does

- It raises the effective value of medium Spades into boss-relevant mitigation.
- It makes low Spades (2-4) less dead by turning them into meaningful defense pieces.
- It smooths variance: weak hands can still contribute through shield stacking.

### Timing window

Best use cases:
- early in long enemy fights where the enemy is likely to survive
- before a known high-risk counterattack
- when a teammate has high-value but brittle hand composition

Worst use cases:
- on lethal turns where the enemy dies before attacking
- too early when route/camp resources would have solved the same threat

## Specialization Paths

## Bulwark — Protects Allies

First Spade played each enemy also reduces enemy attack by 1 for this turn.

### Effect profile

- immediate mitigation on the current attack check
- strongest versus Queens/Kings and route elites with inflated attack
- scales with all shield sources because it reduces pre-shield base attack

### Interactions

- Quartermaster: stronger draw access to Spades makes Bulwark near-always online.
- Surgeon: lower immediate damage reduces emergency healing burden.
- Boss preparations: stacks well with one-time boss cripple, often converting lethal checks into manageable discards.

### Best play pattern

Road:
- trigger on enemies expected to survive at least one cycle.
- prioritize preserving mid-value cards for future discard checks.

Camp:
- plan around who likely eats next counterattack and reserve Spades in that hand.

Boss:
- front-load Bulwark trigger on first dangerous turn of each phase.

## Guardian — More Shield

When net attack is reduced to 0, one ally may discard one card and draw one card.

### Effect profile

- converts defensive success into hand quality improvement
- creates tempo-neutral filtering, which is rare and powerful
- strongest in long fights with repeat zero-net turns

### Interactions

- Exile: Guardian helps fix awkward post-thinning hand states.
- Oracle: deck reorder plus zero-net loops can repeatedly sculpt ideal hands.
- Gambler: safer wagers when the party can recover card quality for free.

### Best play pattern

Road:
- engineer zero-net turns intentionally instead of always chasing kills.
- use free filter to clear low-value dead cards before elite nodes.

Camp:
- pick route commitments that provide recurring Spade access.

Boss:
- treat zero-net turns as setup turns for exact-kill or prep windows.

## Strategic Interactions

Suit interactions:
- Spades: direct scaling and class signature.
- Hearts: Sentinel lowers incoming hand erosion, increasing Hearts ROI.
- Diamonds: improves consistency of finding Spades before key turns.
- Clubs: Sentinel enables safer delayed lethal attempts.

Campaign engine interactions:
- Death fork: lower attack spikes reduce forced death-fork entries.
- Camp planning: Sentinel defines when the party can skip stabilization greedily.
- One-time preparations: Sentinel is best when preparations are saved for true spike turns, not routine mitigation.

## Best Team Context

Best alongside:
- Quartermaster for draw consistency
- Surgeon for recovery loops
- Commander for turn control and shield windows

Risky alongside:
- high-variance builds with no draw support
- aggressive overkill plans that ignore attack mitigation windows

## Common Mistakes

- spending the +2 shield on trivial enemies
- overcommitting Spades on turns where enemy already dies
- assuming shield is enough without hand-value planning

## Practical Playbook

1. Identify upcoming attack threshold that can break a hand.
2. Reserve one Spade line for that threshold.
3. Use Sentinel trigger on first truly dangerous enemy cycle.
4. Convert stabilized turns into hand quality via route/camp planning.
5. Enter boss with Sentinel trigger mapped to phase spikes, not random turns.
