# Spells

> 🔁 **Ascending-deck economy (2026-06-15, built).** Spells now come only from **the
> Sanctum** (renamed Abbey) — take **1 of 2**, or pay the **rite** (spend the top 2 of
> your deck) for a **rare** — plus the odd event. The Shrine delivers the **Curse**
> service (cleanse a card's curse; the castable curse spell awaits this revamp). The
> pool is **Kingdom-gated** (`unlockedSpells`): a run starts with **4** and **death
> unlocks more** (`SPELL_UNLOCK_ORDER`). Item stops are **capped at 3/chapter**.

Spells are team-owned tactical resources.

## Canon Rules

- Spells are stored in team inventory.
- Spells are usually immediate one-shot effects.
- Spell sourcing is tied to reward tables and specific landmarks, not camp purchasing.
- Spells can interact with chapter deck state and encounter tempo.

## CT Baseline

- Standard spell value: 0.25 CT
- Rare spell value: 0.75 CT

## Acquisition

Spells come from:
- spell-aligned landmarks
- selected encounter rewards
- risk-weighted rare reward channels

## Design Intent

Spell design should emphasize:
- tactical timing
- chapter-level deck manipulation
- emergency access and conversion options

Example themes:
- exile a card
- boost one card value
- reorder immediate draw quality
- force critical initiative pivot

## Ownership Contrast

- Relics: hero-owned persistent chapter effects
- Preparations: team-owned camp activations for next encounter
- Spells: team-owned tactical tools used for immediate leverage
