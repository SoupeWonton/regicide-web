# Exile

## Class Identity

Tier: 3 — Weird Hero
Theme: Deck Evolution
Guiding question: What should the expedition become?
Interaction layer: campaign state, deck ecology, and future rewards

Exile is controlled deprivation. It manipulates deck composition at real cost and is designed to be powerful but dangerous.

## Expedition Stability Profile

| Pillar | Rating | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Shield | 0 | — |
| Access | 1 | thinner deck improves draw quality |
| Recovery | 0 | — |
| Initiative | 0 | — |
| Consistency | 3 | deck shaping is the highest consistency lever |

CT per chapter: ~0.75 (conditional on discipline)
Specialization bonus: Hollow Archivist +0.25 / Ash Courier +0.25
Note: Exile is the only class that can also reduce CT through misuse. Over-thinning can destroy small-attack discard capacity and generate Burden.
Primary failure mode addressed: hand quality collapse

Core ability:
- Once per camp, exile one Tavern card permanently for this chapter.

Cost rule:
- Every second exile in a chapter adds one Burden token; each Burden makes the next hero death fork harsher (-1 option choice).

Specializations:
- Hollow Archivist: exiling low cards (2-4) grants +1 preparation charge, but Hearts recover one fewer card this chapter.
- Ash Courier: once per enemy, treat one exiled-rank card as wild rank for combo legality.

## What Exile Actually Does

Exile changes the ecology of the deck. It can remove dead weight and improve consistency, but it can also destroy small-value payment flexibility and make discard checks awkward.

Exile is a mastery class. It should not be default for early campaigns.

## Core Ability Deep Analysis

### Exile decision quality

A good exile decision depends on:
- current chapter pressure profile (small vs large attack checks),
- remaining card-distribution needs,
- planned route commitments and support package.

High-value exiles:
- redundant low cards when team has enough low-value discard coverage.
- suits that are overrepresented relative to class plan.

High-risk exiles:
- aggressive low-card removal before known frequent small discard checks.
- removing too many same-rank anchors needed for combo lines.

### Interaction map

Strong interactions:
- Quartermaster Logistician (mitigates composition mistakes).
- Oracle (information improves exile precision).
- Hunter Executioner (precision deck supports exact-kill plans).

Risk interactions:
- Surgeon dependence increases if Hearts recovery is weakened.

## Specialization Path: Hollow Archivist

Text:
- Exiling low cards (2-4) grants +1 preparation charge, but Hearts recover one fewer card this chapter.

### What it does strategically

Hollow Archivist exchanges long-run deck safety for stronger boss planning leverage.

Use Hollow Archivist when:
- chapter rewards preparation more than road stamina,
- team has alternative recovery tools,
- players can avoid over-thinning low-value discard payment cards.

### Interactions

Best with:
- Commander and Merchant prep stacks.
- Sentinel mitigation to offset lower Hearts recovery.

Risk:
- attrition-heavy roads can become lethal before boss.

Best play pattern:
1. Exile selectively, not greedily.
2. Convert gained prep charges into real boss counters.
3. Route for occasional recovery nodes to offset Hearts penalty.

## Specialization Path: Ash Courier

Text:
- Once per enemy, treat one exiled-rank card as wild rank for combo legality.

### What it does strategically

Ash Courier converts exile history into tactical flexibility. It is a puzzle specialization that rewards planning and rank tracking.

Use Ash Courier when:
- party wants adaptive combo construction,
- chapter has many awkward hand states,
- players track exiled ranks carefully.

### Interactions

Best with:
- Commander (initiative and combo flexibility).
- Executioner (improved threshold access).

Risk:
- cognitive load is high; mismanagement loses value quickly.

Best play pattern:
1. Track exiled ranks as tactical resources.
2. Build turns around one key legal-combo unlock per enemy.
3. Preserve hand value by replacing expensive fallback plays.

## Best Way to Play Exile

Road:
- treat each exile as a strategic debt instrument, not free value.

Camp:
- decide exile based on next chapter pressure profile, not immediate frustration.

Boss:
- spend flexibility and prep gains where phase outcomes hinge on exact lines.

## Common Misplays

- over-thinning 2-4 range and losing small-attack payment flexibility.
- stacking Burden without fork contingency.
- choosing Hollow Archivist in chapters with heavy attrition and weak mitigation.

## Build Guidance

Pick Hollow Archivist if:
- boss prep leverage is decisive.
- party can survive weaker Hearts recovery.

Pick Ash Courier if:
- team has strong tactical players.
- campaign values adaptive combo precision.

## Difficulty Impact

Compared to base game:
- can lower perceived difficulty for experts.
- increases failure rate for undisciplined play.
