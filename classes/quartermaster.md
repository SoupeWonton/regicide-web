# Quartermaster

## Identity

Tier: 1 — Core Hero
Theme: Draw / Access
Suit: Diamonds
Guiding question: How do we keep options?

The Quartermaster is the expedition's consistency engine. This class does not win by damage; it wins by improving card flow, reducing dead turns, and increasing the quality of every other class.

## Expedition Stability Profile

| Pillar | Rating | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Shield | 0 | — |
| Access | 3 | primary function |
| Recovery | 0 | — |
| Initiative | 0 | — |
| Consistency | 1 | filtering reduces hand-quality collapse |

CT per chapter: ~1.0 to ~1.25 (tuning sensitive to Diamond trigger frequency)
Specialization bonus: Logistician +0.5 / Merchant +0.5
Primary failure mode addressed: hand quality collapse and suit access gaps

Core role:
- stabilize draw economy
- convert Diamonds into team-wide agency
- protect against hand stagnation before boss checkpoints

## Core Ability

The first Diamond trigger each enemy draws +1 extra total card.

### What it actually does

- increases the chance each encounter creates at least one functional hand fix
- makes small Diamond plays meaningful even when base attack is low
- improves late-chapter survivability by reducing no-option turns

### Timing window

Best use cases:
- when multiple players are below hand cap
- when one player is at immediate discard risk
- in road nodes where card quality has degraded from attrition

Worst use cases:
- when everyone is capped and draw is wasted
- on turns where lethal is guaranteed and draw has no downstream value

## Specialization Paths

## Logistician — Deck Control

After a Diamond draw, one player may discard 1 and draw 1.

### Effect profile

- precision filtering layered on top of raw draw
- removes dead cards without sacrificing initiative
- excellent for preparing exact-kill or shield timing hands

### Interactions

- Executioner: improves threshold damage setups.
- Surgeon: reduces pressure to use Hearts as emergency cleanup.
- Exile: mitigates high-variance deck composition after thinning.

### Best play pattern

Road:
- target player with worst hand quality, not biggest hand size.
- use filter to remove low impact cards before elite forks.

Camp:
- prioritize route commitments that reward additional draw/filter lines.

Boss:
- use first Diamond cycle for setup, not panic recovery.

## Merchant — Economy

At camp, grant one extra preparation slot for the next boss.

### Effect profile

- direct power increase in planning phase
- amplifies value of one-time preparations and rare prep rewards
- strongest in chapters where boss modifiers are harsh

### Interactions

- Camp planning layer: Merchant scales with every preparation decision.
- Oracle/Commander: more prep options increase execution flexibility.
- Gambler: extra prep slots de-risk high-variance bets.

### Best play pattern

Road:
- bias toward preparation rewards over immediate comfort when stable.

Camp:
- treat extra slot as mandatory strategic edge; never leave it empty.

Boss:
- pair one defensive prep and one initiative prep for phase control.

## Strategic Interactions

Suit interactions:
- Diamonds: signature scaling.
- Spades: better draw means more timely mitigation.
- Hearts: draw and recovery together sustain long road chains.
- Clubs: improves probability of finding threshold damage cards.

Campaign engine interactions:
- Death fork: better hand flow lowers death incidents.
- Camp planning: Quartermaster makes camp decisions matter more by increasing available levers.
- Route commitment: strongest in resource routes (Abbey/merchant style lines).

## Best Team Context

Best alongside:
- Sentinel for defensive reliability
- Executioner for cleaner kill turns
- Commander for sequence control

Risky alongside:
- all-in damage teams that skip hand management
- thin decks without enough fallback draw lines

## Common Mistakes

- using Diamonds only as panic response
- filtering wrong player due to short-term focus
- taking immediate reward over prep slot value when Merchant is online

## Practical Playbook

1. Evaluate which hand fails next, not which hand looks best now.
2. Trigger first Diamond when it fixes multiple future turns.
3. Filter dead cards from highest-risk player first.
4. At camp, convert economy into preparation depth.
5. Enter boss with explicit draw plan per phase.
