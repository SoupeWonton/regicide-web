# Catastrophe Tolerance (CT) System

Version 1.0

## Purpose

Catastrophe Tolerance (CT) is the primary balancing metric used to evaluate:

- campaign progression
- encounter difficulty
- reward value
- class power
- chapter pressure

CT is a design and debugging tool.

CT is never shown to players.

Players experience CT indirectly through survivability, recovery opportunities, information, flexibility, and strategic options.

The core CT question is:

> How many meaningful mistakes, bad situations, or adverse outcomes can the expedition recover from before failing?

## Core Principle

Every chapter creates pressure.

Every player reward creates tolerance.

Difficulty emerges from the relationship between the two.

The campaign should not be balanced through intuition alone.

All content should eventually translate into CT.

## CT Equation

Net Difficulty = Total Pressure CT - Total Tolerance CT

### Total Pressure CT

Road Pressure CT + Boss Pressure CT

### Total Tolerance CT

Class CT + Specialization CT + Relic CT + Spell CT + Preparation CT + Memory CT

The objective is not to eliminate difficulty.

The objective is to maintain a desired pressure gap.

## Pressure CT

Pressure CT represents problems generated by the game.

Examples:

- incoming attack pressure
- discard pressure
- initiative pressure
- information pressure
- attrition pressure
- deck starvation pressure

Pressure CT is generated by:

- encounters
- elites
- bosses
- chapter modifiers

Pressure CT is always a cost and is never earned by players.

## Tolerance CT

Tolerance CT represents recovery opportunities.

Tolerance CT does not necessarily prevent mistakes.

Tolerance CT creates opportunities to recover from mistakes.

Examples:

- extra shield
- extra draw
- information
- initiative control
- deck filtering
- death mitigation

Tolerance CT is earned through progression systems.

## Standard CT Values (Initial Targets)

These values are starting design targets and may change after playtesting.

### Class

Base class = 0.75 CT.

Core examples:

- Sentinel
- Quartermaster
- Surgeon
- Executioner

Standard 4-player starting party:

4 x 0.75 = 3.0 CT.

### Specialization

Hero specialization = 0.75 CT.

Specialization should approximately double hero contribution.

Specialized hero contribution:

0.75 + 0.75 = 1.5 CT.

### Memory

Standard memory = 0.25 CT.

### Relics

Standard relic = 0.25 CT.

Rare relic = 0.75 CT.

### Spells

Standard spell = 0.25 CT.

Rare spell = 0.75 CT.

### Preparations

Standard preparation = 0.25 CT.

Rare preparation = 0.75 CT.

Preparations are activated during camp and apply to the next encounter.

## Encounter Pressure Model

Encounters generate Pressure CT.

Pressure CT is estimated from:

Pressure CT = Survival Pressure + Attrition Pressure + Initiative Pressure + Special Rule Pressure

This formula is intentionally abstract. Designers assign values after testing.

Initial pressure targets:

- Road Encounter: about 0.25 CT
- Hard Road Encounter: about 0.5 CT
- Elite Encounter: about 0.75-1.0 CT
- Mini Boss: about 1.5 CT
- Boss: about 4-8 CT

These are tuning targets, not player-facing stats.

## Landmark Evaluation

Every landmark is evaluated using net CT gain.

Net CT Gain = Reward CT - Travel Pressure CT

Examples:

- Forge: Reward 1.0, Travel 0.5, Net +0.5 CT
- Lair: Reward 2.0, Travel 1.25, Net +0.75 CT

This keeps different route identities viable while retaining balance parity.

Detailed landmark-by-landmark reward taxonomy and tuning guidance are defined in ../systems/landmarks.md.

## Chapter Construction

Each chapter defines:

- pressure budget
- expected CT budget

Illustrative targets:

- Chapter 1: Road 1 + Boss 5 = 6 Pressure CT, Expected Party CT about 5, outcome manageable but tense
- Chapter 2: Road 2 + Boss 8 = 10 Pressure CT, Expected Party CT about 8, outcome difficult but fair

## CT Design Rule

For every new content element, the designer must answer:

- How much CT does this generate?
or
- How much CT does this provide?

If that answer is unclear, the content is not ready for balancing.

## CT Categories

Every source of CT should primarily map to one category.

These are classification tags, not separate currencies.

### Shield

Examples: Spades, Sentinel.

### Access

Examples: Diamonds, Quartermaster.

### Recovery

Examples: Hearts, Surgeon.

### Initiative

Examples: Executioner, Commander.

### Consistency

Examples: deck shaping, information, prediction.

## Final Rule

Players never see CT.

Designers use CT to:

- evaluate classes
- evaluate rewards
- evaluate landmarks
- evaluate bosses
- evaluate chapter difficulty
- compare alternative designs

CT is the shared balancing language for the campaign.

If Chapter 2 feels too difficult after testing, adjust CT values before redesigning systems.
